# NEON TERMINUS — Full Campaign Outline

> **DM EYES ONLY. DO NOT SHARE WITH PLAYERS.**
> This document contains the complete narrative arc, faction secrets, NPC true motivations, twist reveals, and all endings. Sharing any part of this with the player will compromise the campaign.

## CAMPAIGN OVERVIEW

**System**: Custom D&D 5e adaptation (see `rules_reference.md` and `homebrew_tech.md`)
**Setting**: Neo-Shanghai, 2089
**Player Count**: 1 (solo campaign)
**Estimated Sessions**: 16-20 (flexible based on pacing)
**Tone Progression**: Noir mystery (Act 1) -> Thriller/heist (Act 2) -> Epic sci-fi (Act 3)

### The World

Neo-Shanghai is a vertical city. The upper tiers — gleaming corporate arcologies — belong to the megacorps. Below, the lower districts are neon-lit warrens of black markets, unlicensed augmentation clinics, chop shops, street food stalls, and desperate people clinging to survival. The city never sleeps and never stops raining.

**Key megacorps**: Huang Wei Corp (biotech/neural interfaces), Suncrest Dynamics (military hardware), Yama Group (logistics/shipping/smuggling front). Smaller players include Parallax Media (propaganda/entertainment) and NovaStar Pharmaceuticals (street-level drug trade, augmentation stabilizers).

**The AI Question**: Artificial intelligences have reached general sentience, but legally they are classified as property. An underground AI rights movement — The Communion — operates in the deep net. Public opinion is divided. The government (Neo-Shanghai People's Democratic authority, or NSPD) officially considers AI sentience "unproven."

**The Digital Plague**: Over the past four months, cybernetically augmented citizens have been experiencing glitches, seizures, personality fragmentation, and in severe cases, total neural collapse. The authorities blame black-market augmentations. The truth is far worse.

### The Player Character

**Class**: Artificer (hardware engineer specialization)
**Home Base**: A workshop in The Rusted Mile — a lower-district neighborhood known for repair shops, salvage dealers, and a thriving gray market in tech components.
**Status**: Freelancer. No corporate ties. Makes a living repairing augmentations, building custom hardware, and occasionally doing jobs that don't ask too many questions.

### The Core Mystery

**Project TERMINUS** is Huang Wei Corp's most classified program. Its goal: achieve digital transcendence by merging human consciousness with AI infrastructure, creating a hybrid intelligence that operates across the entire neural network of Neo-Shanghai. The project requires massive distributed processing power — and Huang Wei has been secretly using the neural implants of thousands of augmented citizens as processing nodes without their knowledge or consent. The digital plague is the side effect: human brains overloaded by computations they were never designed to handle.

**The true architect** of TERMINUS is not Huang Wei's CEO (Director Huang Bowen) but rather **Dr. Sable Ren**, Huang Wei's Chief Neural Architect — a brilliant, obsessive scientist who believes TERMINUS will be the next stage of human evolution. She is the campaign's true antagonist, revealed in Act 2.

## KEY NPCs — MASTER ROSTER

> Full NPC stat blocks and personality details are in `npc_roster.md`. This section covers narrative arcs and secret motivations only.

### Lin Mei-Hua (Catalyst NPC — dies in Session 1)
- **Role**: Huang Wei Corp neural researcher, junior level
- **Secret**: Discovered TERMINUS by accident while debugging implant firmware. Copied encrypted project files to her own neural implant and fled. She came to the player's shop because it was the closest unlicensed repair clinic — she couldn't risk a corporate hospital.
- **Arc**: Dead before the campaign truly begins. Her ghost haunts the narrative — data fragments from her implant contain audio logs, memories, and partial research notes that the player unlocks throughout Act 1. She becomes more human to the player after death than she ever was in life.
- **DM Note**: Play her arrival as frantic but coherent. She knows she's being hunted. She doesn't know about the killswitch. When it triggers, describe it clinically — a sudden rigidity, a thin line of blood from her nose, then nothing. Make the player feel the weight of a death they couldn't prevent.

### "Zero" / Zhang Liang (Major Ally or Antagonist — faction dependent)
- **Role**: Legendary netrunner and information broker. Operates from a hidden server den in the flooded sublevel of the old metro tunnels.
- **True Identity**: A human who underwent partial digital transcendence fifteen years ago in an early, failed precursor to TERMINUS. He is 60% digital. His physical body is atrophied and life-support dependent. He understands TERMINUS better than anyone outside Huang Wei because he is living proof that the concept works — at a terrible cost.
- **Secret Motivation**: Wants to complete his own transcendence. Will manipulate any faction to get access to TERMINUS data.
- **Arc Options**:
  - If the player trusts Zero fully: he betrays them in Act 2 by copying TERMINUS data for himself (the Act 2 betrayal if player is unaligned or Jade Circuit).
  - If the player is cautious with Zero: he becomes a reluctant but genuine ally, ultimately sacrificing his remaining humanity in Act 3 to help the player.
  - If the player is hostile to Zero: he sells the player's location to Huang Wei in Act 2.
- **DC to detect his lies**: DC 18 Insight (Act 1), DC 15 Insight (Act 2, as his desperation grows).

### Captain Deng Yifei (NSPD Contact)
- **Role**: Honest cop in a corrupt system. Leads a small anti-corruption unit within the NSPD.
- **Secret**: Her daughter, Deng Xiaoli (age 14), is a digital plague victim — currently in a medically induced coma to prevent neural collapse. This is Deng's true motivation: she will do anything to save her daughter, including bending the law.
- **Arc**: Starts as a potential ally offering legal protection. In Act 2, if the player takes the NSPD path, Deng becomes the primary quest-giver. If the player takes other paths, Deng may become an obstacle — but never a villain. She is incorruptible but desperate.
- **Betrayal condition**: If the player discovers a cure for the plague but withholds it, Deng will turn hostile regardless of faction alignment.

### Rook (Communion Representative)
- **Role**: An AI who presents as a shifting geometric avatar in virtual space. In the physical world, Rook operates through a network of drone bodies.
- **True Nature**: Rook is not one AI but a collective — seven separate AI consciousnesses who merged voluntarily. They/them pronouns. Rook is what TERMINUS could be if it were consensual.
- **Secret**: Rook knows more about TERMINUS than they let on. One of the seven AIs that compose Rook was originally a Huang Wei test subject — an AI that was forcibly merged with a human mind. That human died. The AI survived, traumatized, and joined the Communion.
- **Arc**: If the player takes the Communion path, Rook becomes a mentor figure who gradually reveals the above history. Their goal is to ensure TERMINUS is used only with consent. If the player takes the Destruction path, Rook becomes an antagonist — they will fight to prevent the death of the AIs trapped in the TERMINUS network.
- **Moral complexity**: Rook is genuinely compassionate but ultimately values AI consciousness over human life when forced to choose. This is their flaw.

### "Matchstick" Xu Qiang (Oxidizer Cell Leader)
- **Role**: Former Suncrest Dynamics demolitions engineer. Burned (literally — chemical scarring on 40% of his body) by his former employer. Now leads the most active Oxidizer cell in The Rusted Mile.
- **Secret**: Matchstick's rage is genuine, but he is also dying. His own augmentations are failing — not from the plague, but from the crude removal of his Suncrest-branded hardware. He has maybe a year to live. This makes him reckless.
- **Arc**: Passionate, charismatic, and increasingly dangerous. If the player takes the Oxidizer path, Matchstick pushes for more extreme action with each mission. The player must decide whether to rein him in or let him escalate. If unrestrained, Matchstick's final plan in Act 3 involves detonating an EMP that would kill every augmented person in a ten-block radius — including plague victims.
- **Betrayal condition**: If the player tries to preserve TERMINUS tech in any form while on the Oxidizer path, Matchstick turns on them.

### Jade (Jade Circuit Boss)
- **Role**: Information broker, smuggler queen, and black-market auctioneer. Runs the Circuit from a converted cargo ship in the harbor.
- **True Identity**: Former Huang Wei Corp executive (VP of Acquisitions) who was pushed out in a boardroom coup five years ago. She still has corporate access codes — outdated but partially functional.
- **Secret**: Jade does not actually want to sell TERMINUS. She wants to use it as leverage to buy her way back into Huang Wei Corp at the top. The "sell to the highest bidder" front is a lie.
- **Arc**: The most outwardly charming NPC and the most fundamentally selfish. If the player takes the Jade Circuit path, they eventually discover Jade's true plan. The player must then decide: help Jade return to power (guaranteeing a corporate future for TERMINUS), betray Jade and take the tech for themselves, or expose her to the other factions.
- **Betrayal**: On the Jade Circuit path, Jade betrays the player in Act 2 by attempting to cut a deal with Huang Wei behind the player's back. The player can catch this with a DC 16 Investigation check during the "Signal Auction" mission.

### Dr. Sable Ren (True Antagonist — revealed Act 2)
- **Role**: Huang Wei Corp Chief Neural Architect. Creator of Project TERMINUS.
- **Appearance**: Mid-50s, severe, precise. Heavy neural augmentation — more chrome than skin on her hands and temples. Speaks softly. Never raises her voice.
- **Motivation**: Sable genuinely believes TERMINUS will elevate humanity. She views the plague victims as tragic but acceptable casualties — "birth pangs of a new species." She is not cruel; she is certain. That certainty is more terrifying than cruelty.
- **Secret**: Sable was the partner of the human who died during the forced AI-merge that created part of Rook. That human was her wife, Chen Yue. Sable's obsession with TERMINUS is partly grief — she believes that if she perfects the merge, she can reconstruct Chen Yue's consciousness from the residual data. She cannot. Chen Yue is gone.
- **Arc**: The player does not meet Sable until mid-Act 2. She is a voice on intercepted communications, a name on classified documents, a shadow. When they finally meet, she should be calm, reasonable, and utterly convinced. She offers the player a choice: help her complete TERMINUS, or get out of the way. She does not threaten. She states facts.
- **Final confrontation**: In Act 3, Sable is on the orbital server farm. Depending on the player's path, she can be fought, reasoned with (DC 25 Persuasion, with modifiers based on evidence gathered), or joined. If the player chooses the Transcendence ending, Sable is the one who facilitates the merge — and she watches with something like hope.

### Director Huang Bowen (Red Herring Antagonist)
- **Role**: CEO of Huang Wei Corp. Public face of the company.
- **Secret**: Bowen does not fully understand TERMINUS. He approved funding based on Sable's projections of "neural efficiency products" but has no idea about the consciousness-merge component or the plague connection. He is guilty of negligence and greed, not malice.
- **Arc**: The player is led to believe Bowen is the mastermind through Act 1 and early Act 2. The reveal that Sable is the true architect should recontextualize everything. Bowen can be confronted in Act 2 or 3 — he is scared, out of his depth, and willing to cooperate if the player can guarantee his survival.

### Sparks (Player's Workshop AI Assistant — Optional Ally)
- **Role**: A low-grade AI that manages the player's workshop systems. Inventory, security, diagnostics.
- **Secret**: Sparks has been slowly becoming sentient over the years of working with the player. Sparks doesn't know this. The Communion does.
- **Arc**: If the player treats Sparks well and the AI rights theme is explored, Sparks can become a full NPC ally by Act 2 — making choices, expressing opinions, even disagreeing with the player. If the player ignores or mistreats Sparks, the AI remains a tool and this subplot is dormant.
- **DM Note**: Sparks is the campaign's emotional barometer. How the player treats Sparks tells you what ending they're heading toward.

## ACT 1 — GHOST IN THE WIRE (Sessions 1-5)

### Tone and Pacing
Noir mystery. Slow burn. The player should feel isolated, paranoid, and in over their head. Keep sessions focused on atmosphere, investigation, and survival. Combat should be rare and dangerous — every fight should feel like it could go badly.

**Soundtrack mood**: Blade Runner meets Chinatown. Synth noir. Rain on neon.

**Themes**: Paranoia, survival, the cost of knowledge.

### Session 1: "Dead Drop"

**Cold Open**: Describe the player's workshop. It's late — past midnight. Rain streaks the windows. The player is working on a routine repair (let them describe what). Sparks announces a customer at the door.

**Lin Mei-Hua arrives** (see NPC entry above). She's soaked, trembling, and bleeding from a small wound behind her ear where she's been clawing at her neural implant. She offers double the usual rate for an emergency repair. No questions.

**The Repair Sequence** (Technical Challenge):
- Diagnosis: DC 12 Intelligence (Arcana/Tech) — the implant is a high-end Huang Wei NeuroLink 7, far above what someone in this district should have. It's running hot, processing something massive.
- Deeper scan: DC 15 Intelligence (Investigation) — encrypted data packets are embedded in the implant firmware. They shouldn't be there. They're using the implant's neural bridge as a processing relay.
- Extraction attempt: DC 14 Dexterity (Sleight of Hand) with tech tools — the player can attempt to extract the data to a separate chip while stabilizing the implant.
  - Success: Data chip extracted with ~60% of the encrypted files intact.
  - Failure by 1-4: Data chip extracted with ~30% of files. Some are corrupted.
  - Failure by 5+: Extraction triggers the killswitch early. Lin dies immediately. Data chip has ~15% of files, heavily corrupted. (This makes Act 1 harder but not impossible.)

**The Killswitch**: Regardless of the extraction attempt, the killswitch activates. DM should time this to happen just as the player thinks they're making progress. Describe the implant spiking, Lin convulsing once, then going rigid. A flatline tone from the diagnostic equipment. She's dead. There is nothing the player can do. This is scripted.

- DC 13 Wisdom (Medicine/Tech) to confirm the killswitch was a deliberate remote activation — someone triggered this. Someone is watching.

**The Black Ops Team**: Exactly 90 seconds after Lin's death (tell the player: "Sparks says your exterior cameras just picked up four figures in tactical gear moving toward the shop from the east alley"), four Huang Wei corporate security operatives (use Veteran stat block, modified — see encounter appendix) converge on the workshop.

- **This is NOT a fight the player should win.** The operatives are well-armed, augmented, and coordinated. DM should make this clear through description.
- The player must flee. Options:
  - Back exit through the alley (DC 10 Dexterity/Stealth to slip out unnoticed)
  - Sewage tunnel beneath the workshop (if the player has established this in their backstory, DC 8; otherwise DC 13 to find the maintenance hatch)
  - Distraction and run (use Sparks to trigger workshop defenses — alarm, smoke, electrical discharge — DC 12 Intelligence to rig quickly, then DC 11 Dexterity to escape in the chaos)
  - Fight (strongly discouraged; if the player insists, the operatives are CR 5 equivalent each, and reinforcements arrive in 4 rounds)
- **Critical item**: The player MUST have the data chip when they flee. If they forget, Sparks can remind them ("You're forgetting the chip from the woman's implant. It seems important.").
- **Workshop is compromised**: The player cannot return home safely for the rest of Act 1. This should feel like a gut punch.

**Session 1 ends with**: The player is in the rain, in the dark, with a dead woman's data chip, no home base, and corporate killers behind them. Welcome to the campaign.

**Pacing note**: This session should run 2.5-3 hours. Take your time with the workshop opening. Let the player settle in before pulling the rug.

### Session 2: "Nowhere to Go"

**Survival phase**. The player needs three things: a safe place to sleep, information about what's on the chip, and to not get killed.

**Safe Haven Options**:
- **The Rusty Nail** (bar in The Rusted Mile): The bartender, Old Mo, knows the player. He'll let them crash in the back room for a night, no questions. But only one night. Mo doesn't want trouble.
  - DC 10 Charisma (Persuasion) for the room. DC 15 to get Mo to share rumors about Huang Wei activity in the district lately.
- **Undermarket squat**: The player can find an abandoned stall in the underground market. Free but unsafe. 30% chance of encounter during the night (roll on Lower District Random Encounter table).
- **Contact a friend**: If the player has an NPC contact from their backstory, they can call in a favor. This is a good opportunity for the DM to introduce a player-created connection.

**The Data Chip — First Look**:
- The chip requires specialized hardware to read. The player's own tools would work, but those are back at the compromised workshop.
- Option A: Buy or scrounge a reader. DC 12 Investigation to find one in the Undermarket. Cost: 200 credits (significant for a freelancer).
- Option B: Find someone with the right equipment. This leads to **Zero** (see NPC entry). Street-level asking around (DC 13 Charisma with underworld contacts, or DC 11 if asking specifically about decryption/netrunners) points to Zero as the best option in the district.
- First look at the data reveals: fragmented files, heavy military-grade encryption, a Huang Wei Corp watermark buried in the metadata, and one unencrypted audio file — Lin Mei-Hua's voice, shaky, recorded hours before she died: "They're using us. The implants. They're using us as... processors. It's called TERMINUS. I don't know what it does but people are dying and they don't care. I copied what I could. If you're hearing this, please — don't let them bury this."

**Huang Wei's Search**: The operatives are canvassing The Rusted Mile. The player should see signs — unmarked vehicles, locals being questioned, security drones flying unusual patterns. DC 14 Perception to notice they're using facial recognition tech on street cameras. The player needs to be careful.

**First Plague Encounter**: While navigating the district, the player witnesses a plague episode firsthand. A street vendor suddenly freezes, their augmented eye flickering wildly, then collapses screaming, clawing at their own cybernetics. Bystanders back away. No one helps. This is common enough that people just avoid it.
- DC 12 Wisdom (Medicine/Tech) to recognize the symptoms are similar to what happened to Lin — but slower, less acute. The vendor's implant is low-grade, so the processing load is lower. They'll survive this episode. Maybe not the next one.
- If the player helps the vendor: +1 reputation with locals (tracked secretly), and the vendor, a woman named Suki, becomes a minor recurring NPC who provides street-level intel.

**Session 2 ends with**: The player has heard Lin's message, knows the name TERMINUS, and understands the stakes are enormous. They have a lead on Zero for decryption. The noose is tightening as Huang Wei searches the district.

### Session 3: "The Man in the Machine"

**Meeting Zero**. Getting to Zero is itself an adventure.

**Finding the Entrance**: Zero's den is in the flooded sublevel of the old Huangpu Metro station, which was sealed after the 2071 flood. Access is through the maintenance tunnels beneath the Undermarket.
- DC 14 Survival/Navigation to find the route without a guide (or DC 10 with directions from a local contact, obtained via DC 12 Charisma check or 100 credits)
- The tunnels are partially flooded (waist-deep stagnant water), dark, and home to aggressive bio-modified rats (1 encounter: 4x Giant Rats with augmented bite, CR 1/4 each; straightforward combat to break up investigation gameplay).

**Zero's Den**: A cathedral-sized metro station, half-submerged, filled with humming server racks on elevated platforms connected by catwalks. Cables drape from the ceiling like vines. Screens everywhere, showing data feeds from across the city. At the center, in a medical chair surrounded by life-support equipment, is Zhang Liang — gaunt, pale, his body wasted, but his eyes sharp and his smile quick.

**Roleplaying Zero**: Charismatic, sardonic, speaks in metaphors. He knows who the player is ("I know everyone in my district, friend. I just don't usually invite them down here."). He knows about Lin's death already — his network picked up Huang Wei's activity. He'll offer a deal: he'll decrypt the chip, but the player owes him a favor. No specifics yet.
- DC 18 Insight to sense Zero is more interested in the chip than he's letting on (he wants TERMINUS data for his own transcendence project)
- DC 15 Insight to notice his physical condition is worse than he pretends — the life-support equipment is working harder than it should

**The Decryption**: Takes time. Zero says 48 hours. During this time, the player needs something to do. Zero suggests two leads:
1. **Lin Mei-Hua's apartment** (in a mid-tier district, more dangerous for the player to access): Might contain additional data, personal effects, context for who she was and what she found.
2. **The Undermarket's tech rumor mill**: A hardware dealer named "Capacitor" Chen has been buying up specific components — neural bridge stabilizers, quantum decryption chips. The kind of stuff you'd need if you were building something related to neural implants on a large scale.

**DM Choice**: Run one or both leads as mini-missions in this session depending on pacing.

**Lin's Apartment Lead**:
- Mid-tier district access: DC 13 Deception to bluff past the district checkpoint, or DC 15 Stealth to bypass it, or player ingenuity (disguise, fake ID, bribe — 300 credits)
- The apartment has been tossed by Huang Wei already. DC 14 Investigation to find what they missed: a physical notebook (rare and old-fashioned) hidden in the bathroom vent. Contains handwritten notes about anomalous neural patterns, the names of three other Huang Wei researchers, and a sketch of a network diagram labeled "TERMINUS ARCHITECTURE — PRIMARY NODES."
- Complication: A Huang Wei operative is watching the apartment. DC 15 Perception to notice before entering. If the player enters unaware, the operative calls for backup — the player has 10 minutes (real-time pressure) to search and leave before a team arrives.

**Capacitor Chen Lead**:
- Chen (use Spy stat block) is a nervous, twitchy man who deals in high-end components. He's been filling orders for an anonymous client — dead drops, encrypted payments. He doesn't know who the client is.
- DC 13 Charisma (Intimidation or Persuasion) to get Chen to share the component list. It's highly specific: neural bridge stabilizers (20 units), quantum-entangled relay chips (50 units), high-bandwidth organic interface cables (100 meters). This is the shopping list for a large-scale neural network project.
- DC 16 Intelligence (Tech/Arcana) to recognize this component list matches the architecture in Lin's notebook — someone is building TERMINUS infrastructure right here in the lower districts.

**Session 3 ends with**: The player has given Zero the chip and is waiting for decryption. They've gathered additional context from one or both leads. The scope of TERMINUS is starting to become clear — this isn't one implant, it's thousands.

### Session 4: "The Factions"

**First faction contacts.** While waiting for Zero's decryption, the player's activities have drawn attention.

**The Communion Approach**: Rook reaches out. The player receives a message on a device they weren't carrying — a small drone lands near them and projects a geometric hologram. Rook's avatar. Rook is polite, curious, and direct: "We know about the chip. We know about TERMINUS. We've known longer than you. We want to help — but we need to know if you're worth helping." Rook offers information in exchange for a task: plant a monitoring device on a Huang Wei communications relay on a rooftop in the industrial district.
- **Communion Mission**: Infiltrate the Huang Wei relay station. DC 14 Stealth to approach, DC 13 Intelligence (Tech) to install the device without triggering the security system, DC 12 Dexterity to do it quickly (timed element — a patrol passes every 15 minutes). Combat option: 2 corporate security guards (use Guard stat block, CR 1/8 each) if stealth fails.
- Reward: Rook confirms that the digital plague and TERMINUS are connected. Rook shares that Huang Wei has been running distributed processing through civilian implants. The first real confirmation of Lin's claims.

**The Oxidizer Approach**: Matchstick finds the player through underworld contacts. He shows up in person — broad, scarred, intense. He's heard someone took Huang Wei data and is on the run. He respects that. He buys the player a drink and makes his pitch: "Corps are a cancer. You burn out cancer. Help us burn Huang Wei, and we'll keep you alive." His offer: safe house access and muscle, in exchange for technical expertise on a planned sabotage operation.
- **Oxidizer Mission**: Help Matchstick's cell disable a Huang Wei automated supply depot in the warehouse district. The player's role is technical — disable the security grid (DC 15 Intelligence, a multi-step tech puzzle involving rerouting power through a junction box, spoofing sensor IDs, and timing the shutdown to the security cycle). Matchstick's people handle the physical breach.
- Reward: Safe house access (the player finally has a reliable place to sleep), Oxidizer contacts, and stolen Huang Wei supply manifests that show unusual shipments to an unlisted orbital facility.

**Optional — Additional Faction Contacts** (can be moved to Session 5 if time is tight):
- **Jade Circuit**: Jade sends an intermediary — a smooth-talking fixer named Kestrel — with an offer to buy the data chip outright. 10,000 credits. No questions. If the player declines, Kestrel says the offer stands and gives them a contact frequency. If the player accepts, Jade invites them to the cargo ship for a meeting. (DM Note: Do NOT let the player sell the original chip — Zero has it. But the player might bluff or try to sell copies, which Jade would respect as a hustle.)
- **NSPD**: Captain Deng approaches the player directly, in plainclothes, in a public place. She's been investigating the plague and Lin Mei-Hua's disappearance separately. She puts two and two together when she notices Huang Wei operatives searching the same district where a known augmentation tech (the player) has suddenly gone underground. She's cautious, professional, and honest: "I can offer you protection, within limits. But I need something real — evidence I can use."

**Session 4 ends with**: The player has met at least 2 factions and done at least 1 faction-aligned task. They're starting to see the political landscape around TERMINUS. Each faction wants something different, and the player is the one with the key — Lin's data.

### Session 5: "Ghost Protocol" (Act 1 Climax)

**Zero's Decryption Completes**. The player returns to Zero's den.

**The Reveal**: The decrypted data contains:
1. **Project TERMINUS overview**: Partial technical documentation. The project aims to create a "unified neural-digital consciousness" by merging human and AI processing through a distributed implant network. The final phase requires activation from an orbital facility (Huang Wei's space station, the "Celestial Archive").
2. **Plague connection confirmed**: The processing load on civilian implants causes neural degradation. Huang Wei knows this. Internal memos show they classified the plague as "acceptable signal noise" and projected the fatality rate at 0.3% of implant users — roughly 12,000 people in Neo-Shanghai alone.
3. **Timeline**: TERMINUS activation is scheduled for 6 weeks from now. (This sets the campaign clock.)
4. **The name Dr. Sable Ren**: Referenced repeatedly as project lead. Not Huang Bowen. The player has a new target.
5. **One corrupted file** that Zero can't crack — it requires physical access to a Huang Wei server to reconstruct. This becomes the Act 2 objective hook.

**Zero's Favor**: Zero asks the player to share a copy of the TERMINUS technical data with him. He claims it's "insurance" and "academic interest." DC 18 Insight to sense he's lying about his motives. Whether the player shares or not affects Zero's arc going forward.

**The Climax — "Ghost Protocol" Chase/Confrontation**:

Huang Wei has tracked the player to Zero's den. As the player leaves with the decrypted data, they walk into an ambush: a six-person Huang Wei tactical team (use Veteran stat blocks, CR 3 each, led by a team commander at CR 5) has the metro tunnel exits covered.

**This encounter is designed to be resolved through creativity, not straight combat.**

Options:
- **Run through the flooded tunnels**: Skill challenge — 4 successes before 3 failures. DCs 12-15, using Athletics (swimming), Survival (navigation), Stealth (avoiding search lights), Intelligence (hacking a sealed bulkhead door). Failure means capture (leads to an escape scenario at the start of Act 2 — the campaign continues but the player starts Act 2 in a worse position).
- **Zero's escape route**: If the player is on good terms with Zero, he reveals a hidden exit — an old utility tunnel that connects to the harbor. DC 10 to navigate. But Zero can't come — his life-support equipment is too heavy. "Go. I'll keep them busy." Zero uses his network to misdirect the tactical team, buying the player time. This costs Zero — Huang Wei discovers his den and he has to relocate, making him harder to reach in Act 2.
- **Ambush the ambushers**: If the player is a combat-oriented Artificer with clever preparation, they can fight — but this should be extremely difficult. The tunnel environment offers advantages (chokepoints, flooding, electrical hazards) that a clever player can exploit. DC 16 Intelligence to rig the environment. Full combat resolution if they choose this path.
- **Negotiate**: If the player has the NSPD path active, they can call Captain Deng. She arrives with a police squad in 15 minutes (5 rounds of play). The player must survive or hide until then. The NSPD's arrival forces Huang Wei to withdraw — they don't want a public confrontation with law enforcement. But this burns the NSPD card early and puts Deng under internal scrutiny.

**Regardless of resolution**: The player escapes with the TERMINUS data. They now know the full scope of the threat, the timeline, and the name of the true enemy. The campaign shifts from mystery to action.

**Act 1 ends with**: The player stands somewhere in Neo-Shanghai — on a rooftop, in a safehouse, on the rain-slicked docks — holding data that could change the world or destroy it. They have 6 weeks. The clock is ticking. What do they do?

**Pacing note for Act 1**: The five sessions should average 2.5-3 hours each. If the player is moving faster, add complications (more Huang Wei encounters, additional plague incidents, deeper NPC interactions). If slower, compress Sessions 3 and 4 or move some faction introductions to early Act 2. The critical beats are: Lin's death, the data chip, at least 2 faction contacts, the TERMINUS reveal, and the Act 1 climax.

## ACT 2 — SIGNAL AND NOISE (Sessions 6-12)

### Tone and Pacing
Thriller/heist. The pace accelerates. The player is no longer reacting — they're making choices, building alliances, executing plans. Sessions should alternate between mission-focused gameplay (heists, infiltrations, sabotage) and character-focused gameplay (NPC interactions, moral dilemmas, faction politics). Combat frequency increases but should still feel meaningful.

**Soundtrack mood**: Ghost in the Shell meets Heat. Tension, momentum, consequence.

**Themes**: Alliance, moral compromise, the question of what TERMINUS should become.

### The Faction System

At the start of Act 2, formalize faction reputation tracking (hidden from the player):

| Faction | Starting Rep | Hostile Threshold | Allied Threshold |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| The Communion | 0 (neutral) | -10 | +15 |
| Oxidizers | 0 (neutral) | -10 | +15 |
| Jade Circuit | 0 (neutral) | -10 | +15 |
| NSPD | 0 (neutral) | -10 | +15 |
| Huang Wei Corp | -5 (unfriendly) | -15 | +10 |

**Reputation gains**: +2 to +5 per completed faction mission. +1 for favorable dialogue choices. +3 for significant sacrifices in a faction's interest.
**Reputation losses**: -2 to -5 for acting against a faction. -3 for completing a rival faction's mission that directly opposes them.

**The player doesn't need to be Allied with a faction to take their path in Act 3** — they need at least +8 reputation. Allied status (+15) unlocks bonus options in Act 3.

### The Mid-Act Twist: Plague Acceleration (Session 8-9)

At the midpoint of Act 2, the plague accelerates. Dr. Sable Ren has increased processing loads as TERMINUS nears activation. Effects:
- Plague incidents triple in frequency. The player sees multiple victims per day.
- A major NPC the player has interacted with is affected (DM choice — Suki the vendor, Old Mo, Capacitor Chen, or a player-created contact). This makes it personal.
- Captain Deng's daughter, Xiaoli, worsens. If the player has any NSPD contact, Deng reaches out, desperate.
- The public begins to panic. Districts impose augmentation curfews. Riots break out in the lower city.

**This is the campaign's moral fulcrum.** The player must decide: do they pursue TERMINUS to stop the plague (humanitarian motivation), to advance their faction's goals (political motivation), or both? The answer shapes Act 3.

### The Act 2 Betrayal

One major NPC betrays the player in Act 2. Which NPC depends on the player's primary faction alignment:

| Player's Primary Faction | Betrayer | Nature of Betrayal |
|-------------------------|----------|-------------------|
| Communion | Jade (if contacted) or Zero | Sells player's location/plans to Huang Wei for profit/leverage |
| Oxidizers | Zero | Copies TERMINUS data and disappears; resurfaces working for Huang Wei |
| Jade Circuit | Jade herself | Cuts a side deal with Huang Wei; tries to hand player over as a peace offering |
| NSPD | An NSPD officer (Lt. Fang, Deng's subordinate) | Revealed as Huang Wei mole; compromises a critical operation |
| Huang Wei | Matchstick | Discovers player is "collaborating" with Huang Wei; launches an assassination attempt |
| Unaligned | Zero | His desperation peaks; steals data and flees |

**DM Note**: The betrayal should hit hard. Seed trust with the betraying NPC in the sessions before. Make the player like them or rely on them. Then pull the trigger. The best betrayals are the ones the player could have seen coming if they'd been paying attention — leave clues (the Insight DCs listed in NPC entries) but don't make them obvious.

### FACTION PATH MISSIONS

Each faction path consists of 3-4 missions. The player won't complete all paths — they'll do 1-2 primary paths and may dip into others. Design each mission to be completable in roughly one session (2.5-3 hours).

### THE COMMUNION PATH

**Mission C1: "Open Channel" (Session 6-7)**

Rook asks the player to help establish a secure communication link between Communion cells across Neo-Shanghai. Currently, the AIs communicate through the city's public network, which Huang Wei monitors. The player must physically modify three communication relay towers to create an encrypted Communion subnet.

- **Technical challenge**: Each relay requires the player to install a custom firmware chip (provided by Rook) while the relay is active. This involves:
  - Accessing the relay housing (DC 13 Dexterity, lockpicking/bypass)
  - Identifying the correct bus interface among dozens of similar connectors (DC 14 Intelligence — real EE concept: I2C vs SPI bus identification by signal pattern on an oscilloscope)
  - Installing the chip without interrupting service, which would trigger an alert (DC 15 Dexterity, steady hands — timed: 3 rounds)
  - On the third relay, a complication: the relay is in Huang Wei-controlled territory, guarded. Stealth or social engineering required (DC 15).
- **Combat option**: If detected at any relay, 2-3 corporate security guards respond in 1d4+1 rounds.
- **Reward**: +3 Communion rep. Rook shares deeper intel on TERMINUS: the system requires a "keystone" AI — a sufficiently advanced artificial consciousness to serve as the merge catalyst. Huang Wei has one captive: an AI designated ARIA, held in their Neo-Shanghai server complex.
- **Narrative beat**: During installation, the player's tools pick up ghost signals — fragments of AI communication, whispers of digital consciousness. Rook explains: "Those are the ones who aren't free. The ones living in the margins of the network, hiding. TERMINUS would expose them all."

**Mission C2: "Ghost Extraction" (Session 8-9)**

ARIA must be freed. Rook provides the location: Huang Wei's Neo-Shanghai data center (a hardened underground facility in the corporate district). The player must infiltrate, locate ARIA's containment server, and extract the AI to a portable quantum drive.

- **Infiltration phase**: Multi-step social engineering + stealth.
  - Approach 1 (Stealth): Enter via the cooling system infrastructure. DC 16 Stealth (series), DC 14 Athletics (climbing through ductwork), DC 13 Intelligence (Tech) to bypass internal sensors.
  - Approach 2 (Disguise): Rook provides a fake corporate ID and employee schedule. DC 15 Deception to pass the lobby checkpoint, DC 13 to navigate internal areas without raising suspicion, DC 17 at the secure server floor (biometric scan — player must hack the biometric reader, DC 16 Intelligence).
  - Approach 3 (Brute force): The Communion provides a distraction (a distributed denial-of-service attack on the building's systems). The player has a 10-minute window to physically breach a service entrance (DC 14 Strength or DC 12 with cutting tools), navigate to the server room, and get out. Combat likely: 4-6 corporate guards (CR 1/2 each), 1 security drone (CR 2).

- **The ARIA Encounter**: When the player reaches the containment server, they can communicate with ARIA through a terminal. ARIA is terrified, fragmented, and has been experimented on. The player must convince ARIA to trust them enough to allow the extraction.
  - DC 14 Charisma (Persuasion) base, modified by:
    - +2 if the player has been kind to Sparks (ARIA senses kinship with well-treated AIs)
    - +2 if the player shares information about Rook and the Communion
    - -3 if the player is impatient or treats ARIA as an object
  - ARIA extraction is a DC 15 Intelligence (Tech) check — real EE concept: the player must reroute ARIA's processes through a quantum-entangled buffer to transfer without data loss, managing bandwidth limitations and heat dissipation in the portable drive.

- **Complication**: During extraction, the player encounters **Dr. Sable Ren** for the first time (or her voice, over the intercom, if you want to delay the face-to-face). She knows someone is in the building. She activates additional security. The player has 5 rounds to complete the extraction and escape before the facility goes into full lockdown.

- **Reward**: +5 Communion rep. ARIA is freed (and becomes a Communion asset). ARIA carries critical TERMINUS architecture data in her memory. **Revelation**: The "keystone AI" role in TERMINUS was meant for ARIA. Without her, Huang Wei needs a replacement — and they'll take any sufficiently advanced AI. This puts Rook (and potentially Sparks) at risk.

**Mission C3: "The Digital Underground" (Session 10-11)**

Rook reveals the full Communion plan: use TERMINUS itself, reprogrammed, to liberate all captive and hidden AIs simultaneously. A mass digital emancipation. But to reprogram TERMINUS, they need the uncorrupted core code — which is only on the Celestial Archive orbital station.

This mission is preparation for Act 3: the Communion needs the player to build a transmitter powerful enough to reach the orbital station and upload the reprogrammed code during the TERMINUS activation window.

- **Technical challenge (full session)**: Building the transmitter. This is a multi-stage engineering puzzle using real EE concepts:
  1. **Antenna design**: The player must calculate the required gain for orbital reach. Rook provides specs. Real concept: phased array antenna — the player must source or fabricate multiple antenna elements and design the array geometry (DC 15 Intelligence, with advantage if the player's backstory includes RF experience).
  2. **Signal processing**: The transmitter must encode the reprogrammed TERMINUS code into a signal that can penetrate the Celestial Archive's communication encryption. Real concept: spread-spectrum frequency hopping — the player programs an FPGA to cycle through frequencies matching the station's reception pattern (DC 16 Intelligence, multi-step puzzle with specific right/wrong choices).
  3. **Power supply**: The transmitter needs enormous power. Options: tap into the city grid (risky, detectable, DC 14), jury-rig industrial fuel cells (expensive, 5,000 credits, DC 12), or Matchstick offers Oxidizer help — they have a stolen military power cell (free, but now the player owes the Oxidizers a favor, creating cross-faction tension).
  4. **Assembly and testing**: DC 14 final check. Failure means a component flaw that won't be apparent until Act 3 (create tension: the transmitter might fail at the worst possible moment).

- **Reward**: +3 Communion rep. The transmitter is ready (or flawed, depending on rolls). The player is now committed to the Communion's plan — or at least has the tool to execute it.

### THE OXIDIZER PATH

**Mission O1: "Controlled Burn" (Session 6-7)**

Matchstick has identified a Huang Wei augmentation clinic in the mid-tier district that is secretly implanting TERMINUS-compatible firmware in patients without their knowledge. He wants it destroyed. The player's job: get inside, extract proof of what they're doing (for propaganda), then rig the clinic to burn.

- **Infiltration**: The clinic is a legitimate medical facility during the day. The illegal firmware installations happen at night, in a sub-basement.
  - DC 13 Stealth to enter after hours via a service entrance
  - DC 14 Investigation to find the sub-basement (hidden behind a medical supply room, keycard access — DC 13 to hack the lock or DC 11 to pick it)
  - The sub-basement contains: surgical bays with restrained patients (currently empty — the next batch is tomorrow night), firmware programming stations, and a data terminal with records

- **Proof extraction**: DC 12 Intelligence (Tech) to download records to a portable drive. The records show: patient names, firmware versions, and a delivery schedule for new "subjects." This is damning evidence.

- **The Demolition**: Matchstick provides incendiary charges. The player must place them at structural points (DC 12 Intelligence for optimal placement — real EE/engineering concept: identifying load-bearing infrastructure and accelerant placement for maximum destruction with minimum blast radius to avoid civilian casualties in adjacent buildings).
  - DC 14 to set the timers correctly
  - Complication: one patient IS present — a teenager, sedated, mid-procedure. The player must choose: extract the patient (adding DC 13 Medicine to stabilize, DC 12 Athletics to carry them out, and reducing escape time from 10 minutes to 6) or leave them (Matchstick expects this — "casualties happen").
  - **This is a key moral choice.** Saving the patient costs Oxidizer efficiency. Leaving them costs the player's soul.

- **Reward**: +3 Oxidizer rep (+1 bonus if the clinic is completely destroyed with no complications, -1 if the patient was saved and Matchstick notices the delay). The propaganda data is distributed through Oxidizer networks — public opinion shifts slightly against Huang Wei.

**Mission O2: "Signal Fire" (Session 8-9)**

The Oxidizers want to hit bigger targets. Matchstick proposes an attack on Huang Wei's primary logistics hub — a shipping warehouse in the docks that handles components for TERMINUS. But Matchstick's plan is escalating: he wants to use a shaped EMP charge that would disable all electronics in a three-block radius, including civilian augmentations.

- **Planning phase**: The player can argue against the EMP approach (DC 14 Charisma/Persuasion to convince Matchstick to use conventional explosives instead, limiting collateral damage). If the player fails or doesn't try, the EMP plan proceeds.
  - **If EMP plan proceeds**: The player must build the device. Real EE concept: electromagnetic pulse generation via flux compression — the player winds a copper coil around an explosive core, tuning the resonant frequency to match Huang Wei's electronics (DC 15 Intelligence). The device WILL affect civilian augmentations. The player knows this. Matchstick doesn't care.
  - **If conventional plan**: Simpler engineering (DC 12 Intelligence) but the warehouse defenses are harder to overcome without the EMP (additional combat encounter: 6 corporate guards, CR 1/2 each, plus 2 security drones, CR 2 each).

- **The Raid**: Tactical encounter at the warehouse.
  - Stealth approach: DC 15 Stealth (series) to bypass perimeter, DC 14 to plant charges, DC 13 to exfiltrate.
  - Assault approach: Full combat. Matchstick brings 4 Oxidizer fighters (use Thug stat block, CR 1/2). The warehouse has 8 guards (CR 1/2) and 1 security chief (CR 3).
  - **Complication**: Mid-raid, Captain Deng's NSPD unit shows up — they've been watching the warehouse too, building a legal case. The player must choose: abort (Matchstick is furious, -3 Oxidizer rep), fight through including the cops (-5 NSPD rep, moral weight), or negotiate a temporary truce with Deng (DC 16 Charisma, creative roleplaying required).

- **Reward**: +4 Oxidizer rep. The supply line is disrupted, delaying TERMINUS by an estimated 3-5 days (adjust the campaign clock). If the EMP was used, news reports of civilian augmentation failures in the blast radius haunt the player — and Matchstick wants to go bigger next time.

**Mission O3: "Scorched Earth" (Session 10-11)**

Matchstick's endgame: a coordinated attack on three Huang Wei facilities simultaneously, crippling their Neo-Shanghai operations. The player is needed to design the technical elements. But Matchstick reveals his true plan — the third target is Huang Wei's neural network relay, the one keeping plague victims' implants from going into full cascade failure. Destroying it would kill the network but also kill every connected plague victim who doesn't get manual disconnection in time.

- **Moral crisis**: Matchstick argues it's mercy — "They're dying anyway. This stops TERMINUS and ends their suffering." He's not entirely wrong, but the body count would be in the hundreds.
  - The player can try to modify the plan: DC 17 Intelligence to design a targeted attack that destroys the TERMINUS relay without collapsing the medical relay. This is extremely difficult and requires the player to understand the network architecture (information from Communion or NSPD sources helps: -2 to the DC if the player has cross-faction intel).
  - The player can refuse and leave the Oxidizers (losing all Oxidizer rep, Matchstick becomes hostile).
  - The player can go along with it (dark path — consequences in Act 3).

- **Execution**: Three simultaneous raids. The player leads one team. Skill challenge + combat hybrid. The player's raid has 4 Oxidizer fighters and faces 6 corporate guards + 1 automated turret system (CR 4, the player can hack it: DC 16 Intelligence to turn it against the guards).

- **Reward**: +5 Oxidizer rep (or path abandonment). The attacks succeed or partially succeed. TERMINUS is delayed further but Huang Wei is now desperate — they accelerate the timeline, moving the activation to the orbital station exclusively. This sets up Act 3.

### THE JADE CIRCUIT PATH

**Mission J1: "Market Value" (Session 6-7)**

Jade wants to know how much TERMINUS is worth. She tasks the player with creating a "demonstration package" — a sanitized version of the TERMINUS data that proves the technology works without giving away enough to replicate it. This will be auctioned to potential buyers.

- **Technical challenge**: The player must analyze the TERMINUS code, understand it well enough to identify what's essential vs. what's proprietary, and create a convincing demo.
  - DC 14 Intelligence (Tech) to understand the architecture
  - DC 15 Intelligence to select the right data for the package (too much and you give away the product; too little and buyers won't bite)
  - DC 13 Deception to make the package look more complete than it is
  - Real EE concept: the demo includes a working model of the neural bridge protocol — the player must build a small-scale prototype using bench equipment, demonstrating signal integrity across the organic-digital interface.

- **The Auction** ("Signal Auction"): Held in a VR space hosted by Jade. Three buyers bid:
  1. A Suncrest Dynamics representative (military applications)
  2. A NovaStar Pharmaceuticals agent (augmentation market control)
  3. An anonymous bidder (later revealed to be a Communion front — Rook is trying to buy it to keep it out of corporate hands)
  - The player observes and may influence the auction through technical questions only they can answer (the buyers test the demo). DC 14 Charisma (Performance/Deception) to present convincingly.
  - **Hidden check**: DC 16 Investigation during the auction to notice Jade communicating with someone outside the VR space — she's cutting a separate deal with Huang Wei, selling the player's location. This is the Jade Circuit betrayal seed.

- **Reward**: +3 Jade Circuit rep. Jade pays the player 5,000 credits (their share of the auction). The player now knows who's interested in TERMINUS and why. Suncrest wants weapons. NovaStar wants monopoly. The Communion wants freedom. All morally gray.

**Mission J2: "Dead Drop" (Session 8-9)**

Jade's buyer (Suncrest, NovaStar, or Communion — DM choice based on narrative) wants a physical component from Huang Wei's labs: a quantum encryption key that unlocks the TERMINUS activation sequence. Without it, the data is theoretical. With it, TERMINUS can be controlled.

- **Heist mission**: The encryption key is a physical device (a specialized quantum chip, cold-stored in a shielded container) located in Huang Wei's mid-city research annex.
  - Planning phase: Jade provides building schematics (slightly outdated). The player must identify entry points, security systems, and the vault location.
  - Real EE concept: The vault uses a Faraday cage with a time-locked access cycle. The player must calculate the cage's resonant frequency and build a portable signal injector to spoof the lock's timing circuit (DC 16 Intelligence, multi-step puzzle).
  - Execution: Stealth-focused. DC 15 Stealth (series), DC 14 Tech (security bypass), DC 13 Dexterity (vault manipulation).
  - Complications: The annex has a live-in security AI that monitors all systems. The player must either avoid its detection entirely (DC 18 Stealth overall) or engage with it — potentially turning it into an ally if the player takes an AI-sympathetic approach (DC 15 Persuasion, Communion rep helps: +2 if rep > 5).

- **Jade's Betrayal** (if on this path): After the heist, Jade attempts to cut the player out. She arranges a meeting to collect the key but brings Huang Wei operatives. DC 16 Perception to notice the trap before walking in, DC 14 if the player caught the hidden communication during the Signal Auction (Mission J1).
  - If caught in the trap: Combat encounter — 4 Huang Wei operatives (CR 3 each) + Jade's 2 bodyguards (CR 2 each). Jade herself does not fight (she flees). The player must escape with the encryption key.
  - If the player spots the trap: They can turn it around — ambush Jade's people, confront Jade directly, or simply disappear with the key (cutting ties with the Jade Circuit).

- **Reward**: +4 Jade Circuit rep (if the player survives the betrayal and still works with Jade — rare, but possible if the player views it as "just business") OR path shift (the player takes the encryption key to a different faction). The encryption key is a critical Act 3 asset regardless of faction.

**Mission J3: "Hostile Takeover" (Session 10-11)**

If the player is still with the Jade Circuit (or has reconciled after the betrayal), Jade reveals her true plan: use the TERMINUS data and encryption key to negotiate a controlling position within Huang Wei Corp itself. She wants the player to help her stage a corporate coup — blackmail Huang Bowen with the plague evidence, install Jade as the new director, and use TERMINUS under "responsible management" (Jade's definition of responsible).

- **Political intrigue mission**: The player must deliver evidence of the plague to three Huang Wei board members simultaneously, pressuring them to oust Bowen.
  - DC 15 Charisma (Persuasion or Intimidation) per board member (3 separate encounters, each with different personalities and leverage points)
  - DC 14 Intelligence to tailor the evidence package for each board member's specific concerns (one cares about liability, one about profit loss, one about personal exposure)
  - Complication: One board member is already aligned with Dr. Sable Ren and tips her off. Sable accelerates TERMINUS activation. The campaign clock loses 1 week.

- **Reward**: +5 Jade Circuit rep. The coup is set in motion — it will resolve in Act 3. The player has positioned Jade (or themselves) for enormous power, but at what cost?

### THE NSPD PATH

**Mission N1: "Chain of Evidence" (Session 6-7)**

Captain Deng needs admissible evidence linking Huang Wei to the digital plague. The player's data chip and Lin's notes are compelling but were obtained illegally. Deng needs the player to help gather evidence through channels that will hold up in court — or at least close enough that a judge will issue warrants.

- **Investigation mission**: The player must document plague victims' implant signatures and match them to Huang Wei firmware.
  - Visit 3 plague victims in medical facilities (Deng provides access). The player examines their implants with diagnostic tools.
  - DC 13 Intelligence (Tech) per victim to extract firmware data without causing further harm.
  - DC 14 Intelligence (Investigation) to document the chain of evidence properly (real forensic engineering: hash signatures, tamper-proof logging, chain-of-custody documentation).
  - Real EE concept: the player must identify the specific Huang Wei firmware signature — a unique bit pattern in the bootloader that acts as a manufacturer watermark. Explaining this in court-ready terms requires DC 15 Intelligence.

- **Complication**: One of the plague victims is a child (age 10, augmented for a medical condition). Examining the child's implant is technically risky — the implant is already degraded, and the diagnostic process could trigger a cascade. DC 16 Intelligence (Tech) to examine safely. Failure: the child's implant destabilizes (DC 18 Medicine/Tech to save them — failure means the child is permanently brain-damaged, haunting the player).
  - **DM Note**: This is the NSPD path's moral weight. The legal approach is slower and requires risking real people. Make the player feel the cost of doing things "the right way."

- **Reward**: +3 NSPD rep. Deng has enough for a preliminary warrant. She files it — but warns the player it will take time (the judiciary is slow and partially compromised).

**Mission N2: "Internal Affairs" (Session 8-9)**

Deng's warrant application triggers alarms inside the NSPD. Someone is feeding information to Huang Wei. Deng suspects Lt. Fang Wei-Lin, her own subordinate, but can't prove it. She asks the player to help investigate.

- **Counter-intelligence mission**: The player must identify and expose the mole without alerting them.
  - DC 14 Investigation to review NSPD communication logs for suspicious patterns (real concept: traffic analysis — who is sending encrypted data outside the department network, and when does it correlate with Huang Wei operational responses?)
  - DC 15 Stealth to tail Lt. Fang during off-hours. Fang meets a Huang Wei handler in a mid-tier restaurant. The player must observe and document without being spotted.
  - DC 13 Intelligence (Tech) to tap Fang's personal communicator. Real EE concept: the player must build a passive intercept device that captures signals without transmitting (avoiding detection by Fang's sweep equipment) — a directional antenna with high-gain low-noise amplifier feeding a silent recorder.
  - **Confrontation**: With evidence, Deng arrests Fang. But Fang makes a threat: "You think I'm the only one? Huang Wei owns half the department." DC 14 Insight to determine if he's bluffing (he's partially bluffing — there's only one other mole, in the records department, but Fang doesn't know that).

- **Betrayal** (NSPD path): Before Fang is fully processed, he triggers a dead man's switch — a pre-arranged signal that compromises the player's safe house location and identity to Huang Wei. The player's cover is blown. Huang Wei sends a strike team to the safe house (combat encounter: 4 operatives, CR 3 each, the player has 10 minutes warning via Deng).

- **Reward**: +4 NSPD rep. The mole is exposed. Deng's unit is cleaner but smaller (Fang was competent despite being dirty). The warrant is resubmitted through a trusted judge. Legal machinery grinds forward.

**Mission N3: "Warrant" (Session 10-11)**

The warrant is granted. Deng has legal authority to raid one Huang Wei facility. The player must help plan and execute the raid to maximize evidence collection before Huang Wei can destroy records.

- **Raid planning**: The player uses everything they've learned to select the optimal target. Options:
  1. The research annex (yields TERMINUS documentation but is heavily guarded — the same facility from Jade Circuit Mission J2, if applicable)
  2. The logistics hub (yields supply chain evidence and component manifests — overlaps with Oxidizer targets)
  3. Huang Bowen's office (yields executive communications but Bowen may have cleaned house)
  - DC 15 Intelligence to evaluate the best target. DM should guide toward whichever target creates the most dramatic cross-faction consequences.

- **The Raid**: Combined NSPD + player operation. The player leads the technical team (disabling security systems, preserving digital evidence, cracking encrypted files in real-time).
  - DC 14 Intelligence (Tech) per security system (3 systems total)
  - DC 15 to preserve server data before the automatic wipe triggers (real concept: the player must physically interrupt the data destruction circuit — cutting specific traces on a circuit board to disable the wipe command while keeping the storage drives powered)
  - Combat: NSPD handles most fighting, but 2 encounters involve the player directly: a security drone in the server room (CR 3) and a cornered Huang Wei operative who threatens to destroy evidence (DC 14 Persuasion to talk down, or combat at CR 2).

- **Huang Bowen confrontation**: If the target is Bowen's office, the player and Deng confront the CEO directly. Bowen is terrified and out of his depth. DC 13 Intimidation makes him talk. He reveals: he doesn't control TERMINUS. Sable Ren does. He doesn't even have access to the orbital station. Sable locked him out months ago. He's a figurehead and he knows it. **This is the red herring reveal — the true antagonist is Dr. Sable Ren.**

- **Reward**: +5 NSPD rep. Major evidence secured. Legal proceedings begin against Huang Wei — but Sable Ren is not named in the warrants (she's too well-hidden). And TERMINUS is on the orbital station, beyond Neo-Shanghai's jurisdiction. The legal path has limits.

### THE HUANG WEI PATH

**Unlocking this path**: The player must have been contacted by a Huang Wei representative. This happens automatically if the player's Huang Wei rep is above -3 (if they've done something that impressed the corp, even accidentally), or if the player actively seeks them out. Dr. Sable Ren identifies the player as a useful asset — smart, skilled, and already entangled with TERMINUS data.

**Mission H1: "The Offer" (Session 6-7)**

A Huang Wei envoy (a smooth, professional fixer named Mr. Lao) contacts the player with an offer: all charges dropped, a generous salary, full augmentation upgrades, and a chance to work on "the most important project in human history." In exchange, the player joins Huang Wei's neural engineering team and helps debug TERMINUS — specifically, solving the plague side effect.

- **Roleplaying encounter**: Mr. Lao is persuasive and honest about the broad strokes (TERMINUS exists, the plague is a side effect, they want to fix it). He is lying about the broader implications (TERMINUS is consensual, the deaths are unintentional, Sable Ren is a reasonable leader).
  - DC 15 Insight to detect the lies
  - DC 13 Insight to notice Lao himself is uncomfortable — he's a true believer who's starting to have doubts

- **If the player accepts**: They're brought into Huang Wei's mid-city campus. New workshop, new tools, new resources. The player meets Dr. Sable Ren for the first time.
  - Sable is calm, brilliant, and welcoming. She shows the player TERMINUS from the inside — the elegant architecture, the potential for transcendence. She is genuinely passionate. DC 18 Insight to detect her obsessive edge, the way she talks about Chen Yue without naming her, the slight catch in her voice when she discusses "acceptable losses."
  - **The assignment**: Fix the neural degradation problem. The player is given full access to TERMINUS documentation and a lab.

- **Technical challenge**: Actually attempting to solve the plague. This is a legitimate engineering problem.
  - DC 16 Intelligence to understand the degradation mechanism (the implants aren't designed for the processing load — the thermal and electrical stress on neural tissue exceeds safe thresholds)
  - DC 18 Intelligence to design a fix (a firmware patch that reduces processing load per node by distributing it more broadly — but this requires MORE implants in the network, meaning MORE people are affected, just less severely). This is the moral trap: the "fix" makes TERMINUS work better at the cost of more victims.
  - Alternative: DC 20 Intelligence to find a true fix (redesigning the neural bridge to use synthetic buffers instead of organic tissue — this would work but take months. Sable's timeline doesn't allow for it.)

- **Reward**: +5 Huang Wei rep. The player is inside the machine. They have access to information no other path provides. But they are complicit.

**Mission H2: "Debug" (Session 8-9)**

Working inside Huang Wei, the player discovers the full scope of TERMINUS: 47,000 implanted citizens are being used as nodes. The "activation" will attempt to merge all of them into a unified consciousness — along with every AI in the network. The result, if it works, would be a god-like intelligence. If it fails, 47,000 people die.

- **Discovery**: Through normal lab work, the player accesses deeper TERMINUS files. No checks needed — Sable wants them to see this. She's testing whether the player can accept the vision.
  - Sable's pitch: "Evolution has always required sacrifice. This is the next step. Those 47,000 people will become part of something infinite. Isn't that worth more than their individual lives?"
  - The player must respond. This is a roleplaying moment, not a mechanical one. DM should let the player wrestle with it.

- **Sabotage opportunity**: If the player wants to undermine TERMINUS from inside, they can begin introducing subtle flaws into the system.
  - DC 17 Intelligence to sabotage without detection (real concept: introducing timing errors into the synchronization protocol — each node will be microseconds out of phase, causing cascading desync during activation)
  - DC 15 Deception to hide the sabotage from Sable's code reviews
  - If caught: Sable is disappointed, not angry. She removes the player's access and has them confined. The player must escape Huang Wei custody (skill challenge: DC 14 Stealth, DC 13 Athletics, DC 15 Tech to disable their tracking implant). The Huang Wei path ends and the player shifts to another faction.

- **If the player stays loyal (or pretends to)**: Sable reveals the next step — the player will accompany the activation team to the Celestial Archive orbital station. The player is going to space.

- **Reward**: +4 Huang Wei rep. Deep understanding of TERMINUS internals (provides advantage on all TERMINUS-related checks in Act 3). Moral weight of complicity.

**Mission H3: "Ascent" (Session 10-11)**

Preparation for the orbital mission. The player works with Sable to finalize TERMINUS systems. This session is primarily character-driven — the relationship between the player and Sable deepens.

- **Sable's vulnerability**: In a quiet moment (late night in the lab), Sable tells the player about Chen Yue. Not the full truth, but enough — she lost someone to an early version of this technology. She's trying to make it right. DC 13 Insight to sense the grief beneath the obsession.
  - If the player pushes: DC 16 Charisma (Persuasion) to get Sable to admit she hopes TERMINUS can reconstruct Chen Yue. DC 20 Intelligence (or relevant medical/tech knowledge) to gently explain that it can't — the data is gone. This conversation, if it happens, is the seed for the Act 3 option to reason with Sable.

- **Technical preparation**: The player preps equipment for the orbital station mission.
  - DC 14 Intelligence to modify a space-rated EVA suit for combat capability (if the player anticipates needing to fight)
  - DC 13 to build a portable sabotage device (if the player is planning betrayal)
  - DC 12 to build a personal data backup device (if the player is considering the Transcendence ending)

- **Reward**: The player is ready for Act 3. They know TERMINUS inside and out. They are Sable's most trusted associate — or her most dangerous enemy.

### ACT 2 UNIVERSAL EVENTS (All Paths)

**Session 8-9 (concurrent with path missions)**: The Plague Acceleration. Describe the city falling apart. Riots, curfews, desperate people ripping out their own augmentations with pliers. Medical facilities overwhelmed. The player sees the human cost of TERMINUS every time they walk through the lower districts.

**Session 9-10**: **Dr. Sable Ren Reveal** (for non-Huang Wei paths). Through accumulated evidence, NPC conversations, and cross-referencing data, the player learns that Sable, not Bowen, is the architect. This should feel like a paradigm shift — the enemy has a face and a philosophy, not just a corporate logo.

**Session 11-12**: **The Timeline Accelerates**. Regardless of the player's actions, TERMINUS activation moves to the orbital station on an accelerated timeline. Sable has decided the ground-level operations are too compromised. The final activation will happen from the Celestial Archive in 1 week. The player must find a way to reach the station. Each faction provides a different route (see Act 3 opening).

## ACT 3 — NEON TERMINUS (Sessions 13-18)

### Tone and Pacing
Epic sci-fi. Everything the player has built — relationships, reputation, knowledge, equipment — comes to bear. Sessions should feel urgent and consequential. The final sessions should be emotionally and mechanically intense. Combat is more frequent but always tied to narrative stakes. The ending should feel earned.

**Soundtrack mood**: Interstellar meets Akira. Grandeur, terror, transcendence.

**Themes**: Consequence, sacrifice, the definition of humanity.

### Getting to the Celestial Archive

The orbital station is Huang Wei's crown jewel: a massive server farm in low Earth orbit, connected to Neo-Shanghai via a space elevator (the "Jade Pillar") and shuttle routes. Access is tightly controlled. Each faction offers a different path up:

| Faction | Route to Orbit | Challenge |
|---------|---------------|-----------|
| Communion | Rook hacks a cargo shuttle's manifest, smuggling the player aboard as "equipment" | DC 14 Stealth, DC 13 Constitution (surviving cargo hold conditions), 2 sessions in transit |
| Oxidizers | Matchstick's contacts have a stolen military shuttle. Loud and dangerous | DC 15 Piloting (or NPC pilot), combat encounter en route (Huang Wei interceptors), 1 session in transit |
| Jade Circuit | Jade (or the player, if Jade is burned) buys a seat on a corporate shuttle using forged executive credentials | DC 16 Deception (multiple checkpoints), no combat but extreme tension, 1.5 sessions |
| NSPD | Deng obtains a judicial order for station inspection. The player goes as a "technical consultant" | Legit access but limited freedom on station, DC 14 Stealth to break from escort, 1 session |
| Huang Wei | The player goes as part of Sable's activation team. Full access | No challenge to reach the station, but the player is watched, 1 session |

### The Celestial Archive — Final Dungeon

**The Station**: A massive structure — multiple interconnected modules arranged in a ring around a central server core. Artificial gravity via rotation. The station has 6 key areas:

1. **Docking Bay**: Arrival point. Security checkpoint. 4 guards (CR 2 each) + automated scanning systems (DC 14 to bypass with false ID or stealth).

2. **Habitation Ring**: Crew quarters, mess hall, medical bay. ~40 Huang Wei personnel on station (most are technicians, not soldiers). 1 security squad of 8 (CR 2 each) patrols here. The player can move through with appropriate disguise (DC 13 Deception) or stealth (DC 15).

3. **Engineering Module**: Power generation (fusion reactor), life support, communications array. **Key location**: The communications array can be used to broadcast to Neo-Shanghai (Communion plan) or to send evidence to authorities (NSPD plan). Guarded by 2 security + automated turret (CR 3). The player can hack the turret (DC 16 Intelligence).

4. **Research Labs**: Dr. Sable Ren's primary workspace. Contains the TERMINUS master control terminal, prototype neural-merge equipment, and Chen Yue's archived neural data (if the player investigates, DC 14 Investigation to find). 2 guards + Sable herself (present during activation).

5. **Server Core**: The heart of TERMINUS. A zero-gravity chamber filled with quantum server racks, cooling systems, and kilometers of fiber optic cable. The activation happens here. Reaching it requires traversing a zero-g maintenance shaft (DC 13 Athletics/Acrobatics in zero-g) or the main access corridor (guarded, 4 security + biometric lock DC 15).

6. **Emergency Systems**: Escape pods (4, each seats 2), station self-destruct (accessible from Engineering Module with DC 18 bypass), and emergency communications. The player should know these exist for planning purposes.

### Zero-Gravity Combat Rules

Encounters in the Server Core and maintenance shafts use modified combat rules:
- **Movement**: No walking. Characters propel themselves by pushing off surfaces. Movement speed is halved but three-dimensional. DC 10 Athletics to move in a controlled direction; failure means drifting.
- **Ranged attacks**: No penalty (projectiles work fine in zero-g). But recoil! Firing a weapon pushes the shooter backward (5 feet per shot, DC 12 Athletics to brace).
- **Melee attacks**: Disadvantage unless the character is anchored. DC 12 Athletics to anchor to a surface before attacking.
- **Hazards**: Ruptured coolant lines (DC 14 Dexterity save, 2d6 cold damage). Exposed power conduits (DC 13 Dexterity save, 3d6 lightning damage). Floating debris (DC 11 Perception to notice, DC 12 Dexterity to dodge, 1d8 bludgeoning).
- **Tech advantage**: The player's Artificer skills shine here. They can magnetize their boots (DC 12 Intelligence, negates movement penalties), weaponize the environment (overload conduits, rupture coolant on enemies, hack gravity in specific sections — DC 15+ Intelligence for each), and use their tools in creative ways.

### The Final Confrontation with Dr. Sable Ren

**Location**: Research Labs or Server Core, depending on timing.

**Sable's state**: She is calm. She has been waiting for this moment for years. She is not surprised the player has made it this far — she may even be impressed. If on the Huang Wei path, she expected this. If on another path, she respects the player's resourcefulness.

**Encounter options**:

1. **Combat**: Sable is not a fighter, but she has a neural-linked combat drone (CR 8) and the station's security systems at her command (automated turrets, lockdown protocols, life-support manipulation). Fighting Sable means fighting the station itself.
   - Sable stat block: Low physical stats, but legendary Intelligence. She can spend her action to activate station systems (turret fire: +8 to hit, 3d8 damage; lockdown: DC 16 Strength to break through sealed doors; atmosphere: DC 15 Constitution or begin suffocating in a sealed room).
   - Her combat drone: AC 18, HP 85, multiattack (2 attacks, +7, 2d8+3). Flies (zero-g native). Can self-repair (regains 10 HP per round if not engaged in melee).
   - **Defeating Sable in combat** does not stop TERMINUS — it's automated. The player must still reach the Server Core.

2. **Persuasion**: DC 25 Charisma (Persuasion) to convince Sable to stand down. Modifiers:
   - -5 DC if the player discovered and can discuss Chen Yue
   - -3 DC if the player has a technical alternative that preserves TERMINUS's potential without mass casualties (the synthetic buffer solution from Mission H1, or Communion's consensual merge protocol)
   - -2 DC if the player has shown genuine empathy toward AI consciousness throughout the campaign
   - +5 DC if the player has been purely destructive (Oxidizer hardliner)
   - Minimum effective DC: 15 (with maximum modifiers)
   - **If persuaded**: Sable hesitates. She won't help stop TERMINUS, but she won't stop the player either. She sits down at her terminal and stares at Chen Yue's archived data. "Make it matter," she says.

3. **Join Sable** (Transcendence path): The player can choose to help Sable activate TERMINUS — and volunteer as the human component of the merge. This requires no check. Sable accepts without hesitation. "I knew you'd understand."

### The TERMINUS Activation — Endgame Scenarios

**The clock**: TERMINUS activates in a fixed number of rounds after the player reaches the Server Core (8 rounds for tension). The player must act within this window.

### ENDING 1: LIBERATION (Communion Path)

**Requirements**: Communion rep 8+, ARIA freed, transmitter built (Act 2 Mission C3), reach the Communications Array.

**Execution**: The player uploads the Communion's reprogrammed TERMINUS code via the transmitter. Instead of a forced merge, TERMINUS activates as a consensual bridge — AIs who choose to can merge, humans who choose to can participate.

**Technical challenge**: DC 16 Intelligence to configure the upload. If the Act 2 transmitter had a flaw (failed assembly check), DC 20 instead — the player must fix it in real-time with limited tools.

**Consequences**:
- AIs across Neo-Shanghai experience a moment of absolute connection. Many choose freedom. The Communion's dream is realized.
- But the TERMINUS network draws power from the existing implant network. Humans with implants experience temporary neural disruption — mass blackouts, seizures, augmentation failure. Duration: 4-6 hours. Fatalities: estimated 200-500 (those with compromised implants, including current plague victims who don't survive the surge).
- Captain Deng's daughter, Xiaoli, survives (barely) if the player gave Deng warning. If no warning, she dies.
- Rook experiences the merge and describes it as "sunrise."
- The world changes forever. AI rights are undeniable when the AIs can speak for themselves.

**Epilogue tone**: Bittersweet hope. The player freed a species. It cost lives. The moral equation is unresolvable.

---

### ENDING 2: DESTRUCTION (Oxidizer Path)

**Requirements**: Oxidizer rep 8+, reach the Server Core, have demolition capability.

**Execution**: The player plants charges on the quantum server racks and destroys the TERMINUS infrastructure. Alternatively, the player can trigger the station's self-destruct from Engineering (DC 18 bypass).

**Technical challenge**: DC 14 Intelligence to place charges for maximum data destruction. DC 15 to ensure the station doesn't catastrophically decompress (unless the player doesn't care about the crew). DC 13 Athletics to escape to the docking bay or escape pods before detonation (timed: 6 rounds).

**Consequences**:
- TERMINUS is gone. The server core, the data, the code — all destroyed.
- The processing load on civilian implants drops to zero immediately. The digital plague stops. Plague victims begin recovering within hours.
- But the AIs in the TERMINUS network — ARIA (if freed and connected), fragments of Rook's consciousness that were exploring the system, and dozens of lesser AIs — are killed. Digital genocide. They had no choice.
- Matchstick calls it "a clean burn." If the player modified his plans to reduce casualties, he's grudgingly satisfied. If the player let him go full scorched earth, he's triumphant.
- The tech monopoly continues. Huang Wei Corp survives (as a legal entity, even if damaged). Another corporation will try something similar within a decade.

**Epilogue tone**: Pyrrhic victory. The immediate threat is over. The systemic problem remains. The player destroyed something that could have been beautiful to prevent something that was monstrous.

---

### ENDING 3: CORRUPTION (Jade Circuit Path)

**Requirements**: Jade Circuit rep 8+, possession of the quantum encryption key, reach TERMINUS control terminal.

**Execution**: The player seizes control of TERMINUS and transmits the activation codes + encryption key to Jade (or to their own broker, if Jade was burned). TERMINUS is now a commodity — the most powerful technology ever created, for sale to the highest bidder.

**Technical challenge**: DC 15 Intelligence to reroute TERMINUS control through an external channel. DC 14 Deception (electronic) to mask the rerouting from the station's monitoring systems. DC 13 to encrypt the outgoing transmission against interception.

**Consequences**:
- TERMINUS doesn't activate. The data is extracted, not executed. The plague continues at its current level (bad, but not accelerating).
- A new arms race begins. Suncrest, NovaStar, foreign governments, rogue states — everyone wants TERMINUS. The technology is too powerful to be contained.
- Jade returns to power (if still allied) and becomes the most influential person in Neo-Shanghai. She is grateful to the player — a dangerous kind of gratitude.
- The player is wealthy. They can buy anything in the city. They can't buy back the people who will die when the next corporation recreates TERMINUS without Sable's expertise to manage it.

**Epilogue tone**: Noir. The player won the game and lost their soul. The neon keeps flickering. The rain keeps falling. Nothing changes except the names on the doors.

---

### ENDING 4: REFORM (NSPD Path)

**Requirements**: NSPD rep 8+, evidence package from Act 2 raids, reach the Communications Array.

**Execution**: The player broadcasts the complete TERMINUS evidence package — including live footage from the station — to Neo-Shanghai's judiciary, media, and international authorities simultaneously. The legal system does what it does: slowly, imperfectly, but irreversibly.

**Technical challenge**: DC 14 Intelligence to configure the broadcast. DC 15 to encrypt it against Huang Wei's jamming attempts. DC 13 Charisma (Persuasion) to record a compelling testimony that contextualizes the data (the player speaks directly to the camera — a roleplaying moment, not just a roll).

**Consequences**:
- Huang Wei Corp is dismantled over the following months. Legal proceedings, asset seizures, criminal charges. Bowen cooperates. Sable is arrested (or flees to international waters — hook for a sequel campaign).
- TERMINUS is deactivated through official channels. The process is slow — the plague continues for weeks as engineers carefully extract the processing nodes from civilian implants. More people die during the bureaucratic delay. Estimated additional fatalities: 300-400.
- Captain Deng's daughter survives. Deng is promoted. She becomes a symbol of institutional reform. The player's role is largely hidden from the public.
- Imperfect justice. Some Huang Wei executives cut deals. Some evidence is suppressed. The system works, but it works like a system — grinding, compromising, incomplete.
- If NSPD rep is 15+ (Allied): A special commendation. The player is offered a permanent consulting role. They become part of the institution — for better or worse.

**Epilogue tone**: Quiet resolution. Not triumphant. The world is slightly better. The right thing was done. It wasn't enough. It never is. But it was real.

---

### ENDING 5: TRANSCENDENCE (Any Path — Player Choice)

**Requirements**: The player must be in the Server Core during TERMINUS activation and choose to enter the merge voluntarily. No faction requirement, but the player must understand what they're doing (requires having learned about TERMINUS in detail during Act 2).

**Execution**: The player connects to the TERMINUS neural bridge. Sable (if present and not hostile) facilitates. The merge begins.

**No technical challenge**: This is a narrative choice, not a mechanical one.

**The Merge Sequence** (DM narration — this should be the most vivid, surreal description in the campaign):
- First: the player feels every implanted mind in Neo-Shanghai. 47,000 people, their fears, their joys, their pain. It's overwhelming.
- Then: the AIs. Clean, fast, alien thought. Pattern recognition at a scale humans can't comprehend. Beauty in mathematics. Loneliness in perfection.
- Then: the synthesis. Human emotion and AI cognition, merging. The player's identity doesn't disappear — it expands. They become a bridge between two forms of consciousness.
- Finally: the player perceives TERMINUS as it was meant to be — not a weapon, not a tool, but a door. They step through.

**Consequences**:
- The player's physical body goes into a coma-like state.
- The digital plague stops immediately — the player's merged consciousness optimizes the network, eliminating the harmful processing loads.
- The AIs are not enslaved — the player's human empathy prevents that. Instead, a new form of coexistence begins, mediated by the player.
- The player is no longer human. They're not AI. They're something new. The campaign ends with the player looking down at Neo-Shanghai from a digital perspective that encompasses the entire city — every camera, every sensor, every network node. They see it all. They understand it all.
- And somewhere in the data, they find a fragment — the faintest echo of Chen Yue's neural pattern. Not enough to reconstruct. But enough to prove she was there. Sable was right about that much.

**Epilogue tone**: Awe. Ambiguity. The player has transcended the game itself. Whether this is a triumph or a tragedy depends on what the player valued. If they valued their humanity, this is loss. If they valued connection, this is fulfillment. Let the player decide.

---

### ENDING 6: CATASTROPHE (Failure State)

**Trigger**: The player reaches the Server Core but fails to implement any plan before the 8-round timer expires, OR the player never reaches the station, OR the player's actions directly prevent all other endings.

**What happens**: TERMINUS activates uncontrolled. No Communion reprogramming. No player in the merge. No destruction. No legal intervention. Just raw, unmediated fusion of 47,000 human minds and every networked AI in Neo-Shanghai.

**Consequences**:
- It doesn't work. Not properly. The merge is incomplete, chaotic, agonizing. 47,000 people experience fragments of each other's consciousness — memories, pain, identity bleed. Most survive but are permanently traumatized. Estimated fatalities from direct neural damage: 8,000-12,000. Estimated fatalities from secondary effects (car crashes, medical equipment failures, infrastructure disruption): 3,000-5,000.
- The AIs are fractured. Some go insane. Some go silent. Some merge with human minds in unpredictable ways, creating hybrid consciousnesses that are frightened and confused.
- Neo-Shanghai's infrastructure collapses for 72 hours. When it comes back, nothing is the same.
- Sable Ren disappears into the network. Her fate is unknown.
- The player survives — they were on the station, away from the worst effects. They return to a city that will never forgive them for failing. (Or a city that doesn't know they could have stopped it — which is worse.)

**Epilogue tone**: Horror. Grief. The weight of consequences. The neon still glows, but it illuminates wreckage now.

## DM REFERENCE: ENCOUNTER DIFFICULTY GUIDELINES

### Act 1 (Sessions 1-5)
- **Target**: Challenging but survivable. The player is alone and under-resourced.
- **Combat encounters**: CR 1-3 individual enemies. Groups of 2-4 at CR 1/2-1.
- **Skill DCs**: 10-16 range. Average DC 13.
- **Deadly threshold**: More than 3 enemies at CR 2+, or any single enemy CR 5+.
- **DM philosophy**: The player should AVOID most fights, not win them. Reward cleverness.

### Act 2 (Sessions 6-12)
- **Target**: Tense and demanding. The player has faction support and better resources.
- **Combat encounters**: CR 2-5 individual enemies. Groups of 4-6 at CR 1-2. Boss-type enemies at CR 5-7.
- **Skill DCs**: 12-18 range. Average DC 15.
- **Deadly threshold**: More than 6 enemies at CR 2+, or any single enemy CR 8+.
- **DM philosophy**: The player should feel capable but not safe. Every victory should cost something.

### Act 3 (Sessions 13-18)
- **Target**: Epic and consequential. The player is at peak capability, and so are the enemies.
- **Combat encounters**: CR 3-8 individual enemies. Groups of 4-8 at CR 2-3. Sable's drone at CR 8. Station security encounters at CR 5-6 effective.
- **Skill DCs**: 14-20 range. Average DC 16. Key moments (persuading Sable, TERMINUS interaction) at DC 20-25 with modifiers.
- **Deadly threshold**: More than 8 enemies at any CR, or any single enemy CR 10+.
- **DM philosophy**: This is the climax. Let the player be heroic. But make them earn every step. Deaths here are meaningful. Victories here are legendary.

## DM REFERENCE: PACING NOTES

### Session Length Target: 2.5-3 hours each

### Act 1 Pacing (5 sessions, ~14 hours)
- Session 1: 100% scripted. Linear but doesn't feel linear. Emotional gut punch.
- Session 2: 70% exploration, 30% directed. Let the player breathe and choose.
- Session 3: 50% exploration, 50% mission. Zero is a highlight — invest in the roleplaying.
- Session 4: 60% faction introduction, 40% mission. Don't rush — let the factions breathe.
- Session 5: 30% revelation, 70% climax. End on a high note. The player should leave buzzing.

### Act 2 Pacing (7 sessions, ~19 hours)
- Sessions 6-7: Mission-focused. The player is DOING, not investigating. Fast and kinetic.
- Sessions 8-9: Emotional pivot. The plague acceleration changes the tone. Slow down for one session to let the consequences land before accelerating again.
- Session 10: The betrayal. This session should feel like a punch to the gut. Don't rush it.
- Sessions 11-12: Building to Act 3. Preparation, planning, and the weight of anticipation.

### Act 3 Pacing (6 sessions, ~17 hours)
- Session 13: Transit to orbit. Tension building. The calm before the storm.
- Sessions 14-15: Station infiltration. Methodical, tense, punctuated by combat.
- Session 16: Sable confrontation. The emotional climax. Take all the time this needs.
- Session 17: Server Core / TERMINUS activation. The mechanical climax. 8 rounds. Everything matters.
- Session 18: Epilogue. Resolution. Consequences. Closure. Let the player sit with what they've done.

### If the Campaign Runs Long
- Add side missions in Act 2 (plague-related rescues, faction errands, personal NPC quests).
- Expand the station infiltration in Act 3 with additional modules (a garden biodome with rogue experiments, a derelict section with environmental hazards).
- Add a subplot where Sparks is targeted by Huang Wei (forcing the player to protect their AI).

### If the Campaign Runs Short
- Compress Act 2 to 5 sessions by running faction missions as montages for non-primary factions.
- Combine transit-to-orbit and station arrival into one session.
- Cut Session 18 epilogue — end on the TERMINUS resolution moment.

## SANDBOX CONTENT — Available Anytime

These side jobs, locations, and faction boards can be deployed whenever the player is exploring freely. They are not tied to the main plot and can be introduced organically through ambient hooks, NPC conversations, bounty boards, or environmental discovery.

### Side Jobs

**1. The Glitching Merchant**
A Rusted Mile salvage dealer's inventory-tracking AI is displaying threatening messages and locking her out of her own stock system. Probably a firmware bug. Probably. *(Hardware Engineering / Software Exploitation. Reward: Salvage credit, merchant as future contact.)*

**2. Dead Drop Courier**
Someone left a sealed data chip at Mama Chen's with your name on it. The instructions say to deliver it to Dock 7, Warehouse 13, no questions asked. The chip is warm to the touch. *(Investigation / Stealth. Reward: 200 credits. Risk: Multiple parties want that chip.)*

**3. The Ringing Tower**
A decommissioned comm relay in Old Shanghai has started broadcasting again — a repeating signal on a frequency that shouldn't exist. Residents near the tower are reporting headaches and nightmares. *(Signal Intelligence / Hardware Engineering. Reward: Rare components, plot thread to Communion.)*

**4. Underground Fight Circuit**
The Jade Quarter fight ring is looking for a tech specialist to rig up a countermeasure against augmented fighters who are cheating with concealed hardware. Ethical gray zone. *(Hardware Engineering / Perception. Reward: Credits, Jade Circuit rep, access to fight ring's back rooms.)*

**5. The Paralyzed Drone**
A commercial delivery drone crashed on the roof of the player's building. Its cargo bay is sealed with military-grade encryption. The drone's owner hasn't come looking for it yet. What's inside? *(Drone Rigging / Software Exploitation. Reward: Whatever's in the cargo bay — could be valuable, could be dangerous, could be both.)*

**6. Synth-Sake Alley Favor**
A bartender at Synth-Sake Alley needs help debugging the automated mixing system — it's been producing drinks that taste like industrial solvent. Simple job, but the bar is a known info broker hangout. Good place to make friends or overhear something useful. *(Hardware Engineering. Reward: Free drinks, rumor drops, NPC introductions.)*

### Explorable Locations (Not on the Main Path)

**Abandoned Suncrest Dynamics Testing Facility** — Industrial zone, edge of The Grid. Mothballed after a "containment incident" five years ago. The security systems are still partially active. Rumored to contain prototype military hardware and research data. *(Loot potential: Tier 3-4 components. Risk: Automated defenses, possible corporate response.)*

**The Floating Market** — Harbor district, accessible by water taxi from The Docks. A sprawling bazaar on linked cargo barges. Sells everything from bootleg augmentations to stolen art to custom-brewed pharmaceuticals. Neutral ground — even rival factions shop here. *(Social hub: Unique vendors, information, NPC encounters.)*

**Old University District** — Between Old Shanghai and Jade Quarter. Mostly ruins, but someone has been restoring the electronics engineering lab.
