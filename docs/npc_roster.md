## NPC 01 -- CHEN LIWEI ("Old Wire")

**Role:** Independent augmentation mechanic. Unaffiliated.
**Faction:** None (formerly Huang Wei Corp, 20 years ago).

**Appearance:**
A wiry man in his late sixties with oil-stained hands that never quite come clean. His left eye is a first-generation Huang Wei optic -- outdated, flickering amber when he's tired. He wears the same threadbare jacket every day, pockets bulging with micro-tools and solder wire, and moves with the careful slowness of someone whose back gave out a decade ago but who refused to get it fixed.

**Personality:**
- Patient and meticulous -- treats every repair like surgery
- Deeply cynical about corporations but too tired to fight them
- Quietly generous; undercharges clients who can't afford his rates

**Secret:**
Old Wire was a senior augmentation engineer on the early prototypes that became Project TERMINUS. He quit when he realized what the tech would become. Huang Wei believes he's dead. He has a partial schematic of the TERMINUS neural bridge hidden inside a corrupted data chip he wears as a pendant -- he doesn't know it's still readable.

**Motivation:**
Survive. Keep his head down. Fix broken people because nobody else will. He tells himself he doesn't care about the world anymore, but he does.

**Voice:**
Slow, deliberate. Long pauses mid-sentence, like he's checking the words for defects before letting them out. Calls everyone "kid" regardless of age. Drops technical jargon casually -- "Your median nerve relay's firing about forty mil late, that's why your hand shakes." Sighs a lot.

**Location:**
"Wire's Workshop," a cluttered basement shop beneath a noodle restaurant in the Rusted Flats district. Smells like solder flux and sesame oil.

**Relationship to Player:** Neutral, leaning warm. **This is the quest-giver for the Act 1 inciting incident.** Old Wire hires the player for what seems like a routine aug repair courier job -- pick up a specific salvaged part from a contact in the Jade Quarter and bring it back. The job goes sideways. The "part" is a TERMINUS prototype component, and multiple factions want it.

## NPC 02 -- DIRECTOR HUANG MEIFENG

**Role:** Executive Vice President of Applied Neuroscience, Huang Wei Corp.
**Faction:** Huang Wei Corp.

**Appearance:**
Immaculate. Tailored graphene-weave suit, gunmetal gray, no wrinkles. Her hair is pulled back so tightly it looks painful. Mid-forties, but her face is eerily smooth -- top-shelf biosculpting that costs more than most people earn in a decade. Her eyes are natural, dark brown, and that's the most unsettling thing about her: in a city of chrome and neon irises, she kept her own eyes. She wants you to know she has nothing to hide.

**Personality:**
- Controlled and deliberate -- every word is a chess move
- Genuinely believes TERMINUS will save humanity (ends justify means)
- Capable of real warmth, which makes her dangerous

**Secret:**
Huang Meifeng is terminally ill. A degenerative neural condition, maybe three years left. Project TERMINUS isn't just corporate ambition -- it's her escape from death. She intends to be the first subject. She has not told the board. If she dies before TERMINUS is ready, the project dies with her -- she's the only one with full authorization codes.

**Motivation:**
Complete Project TERMINUS before her body fails. She will remove any obstacle -- but she prefers persuasion to violence. Violence is inefficient.

**Voice:**
Precise Mandarin-accented English, almost musical. Never raises her voice. Uses corporate euphemisms that barely mask threats -- "I'd hate for this to become a resource-allocation problem." Asks questions she already knows the answers to. Pauses to let silence do the work.

**Location:**
The Spire, floors 90-95 (Huang Wei Applied Neuroscience division). Occasionally seen at the Pearl Terrace restaurant on floor 112, dining alone.

**Relationship to Player:** Neutral. Will become aware of the player after the Act 1 job goes wrong. May approach with an offer -- polite, generous, and laced with implicit threat.

## NPC 03 -- "JUNKYARD" ZHU PENG

**Role:** Cell leader and demolitions specialist, The Oxidizers.
**Faction:** The Oxidizers.

**Appearance:**
Built like a loading dock worker because he was one. Shaved head, latticed with old scars and cheap subdermal circuitry that glows faint green under his skin when he's angry, which is often. Missing his right ear -- replaced it with a crude audio amplifier that picks up radio frequencies. Wears a battered surplus military jacket covered in hand-stitched patches: slogans, crew names, a cartoon rat holding a wrench.

**Personality:**
- Loud, confrontational, and deeply loyal to his people
- Smarter than he lets on -- plays the thug so enemies underestimate him
- Haunted by collateral damage from past operations but won't admit it

**Secret:**
Junkyard's younger sister, Zhu Lian, works as a low-level data analyst inside Huang Wei Corp. She has no idea her brother is an Oxidizer. He feeds the cell information she mentions casually over dinner, and hates himself for using her. If Huang Wei ever connects the dots, she's dead.

**Motivation:**
Burn Huang Wei Corp to the ground. Specifically, destroy Project TERMINUS -- he doesn't know exactly what it is, but he knows it's big and it's wrong. Protecting his sister is the one thing that might make him hesitate.

**Voice:**
Fast, aggressive, punctuated with bitter laughter. Swears constantly. Mixes Shanghainese slang with English. Calls corporations "the machine" and their employees "cogs." Talks with his hands. "You think they care about you? You're a line item, brother. A line item they can delete."

**Location:**
Rotates between Oxidizer safehouses. Most often found at "The Furnace," a condemned recycling plant in the industrial zone south of the Jade Quarter.

**Relationship to Player:** Neutral, suspicious. The Oxidizers may approach the player after word spreads about the Act 1 job. Junkyard respects action but distrusts anyone who isn't already committed to the cause.

## NPC 04 -- MADAM QIAN YUEHUA ("The Jade Fang")

**Role:** Underboss, Jade Circuit Triad.
**Faction:** Jade Circuit Triad.

**Appearance:**
Sixty years old and carries every year like a weapon. Silver hair worn loose past her shoulders, framing a face that was beautiful once and is now something more interesting -- sharp, weathered, commanding. Her left hand is a masterwork prosthetic, antique brass and jade inlay, custom-built to her specifications. She dresses in dark qipao modernized with armored lining, and she moves through the Jade Quarter like she owns it. She does.

**Personality:**
- Old-school honor among thieves -- her word is iron
- Ruthless when crossed but never cruel without purpose
- Maternal toward her people; cold toward everyone else

**Secret:**
Madam Qian's prosthetic hand contains a hidden data vault with enough evidence to dismantle three Huang Wei Corp board members. She's been holding it for eleven years, waiting for the right moment. She also knows that Old Wire is alive and who he used to be -- she's protected his secret in exchange for free aug repairs for her people. She has never told him she knows.

**Motivation:**
Expand the Triad's influence while maintaining the old ways. She sees corporations as a greater threat than the police. Would consider an alliance against Huang Wei if the terms were right -- but the Triad's interests always come first.

**Voice:**
Low, unhurried, almost gentle. Speaks Mandarin by preference, switches to accented English when she wants to make sure outsiders understand her exactly. Uses old proverbs and then undercuts them -- "The patient tiger catches the deer. But I've never been that patient, so I bought a gun." Doesn't waste words.

**Location:**
"The Jade Terrace," a teahouse and private club in the heart of the Jade Quarter. Back room, second floor, always the same table. Her people are everywhere in the building.

**Relationship to Player:** Neutral. The Act 1 courier job pickup happens in Triad territory. Depending on how the player handles it, Madam Qian may take notice -- for good or ill.

## NPC 05 -- DETECTIVE PARK SOJIN

**Role:** Investigator, NSPD Cybercrime Division.
**Faction:** NSPD Cybercrime Division.

**Appearance:**
Korean-Chinese, early thirties, with the hollowed-out look of someone who sleeps three hours a night and fills the gap with caffeine and spite. Dark circles under sharp eyes. Badge clipped to a rumpled coat that was professional two years and forty wash cycles ago. Carries a department-issue sidearm she's never fired on duty. Her neural interface port, visible behind her left ear, is a standard NSPD model -- she refused the corporate upgrade the division offered. That tells you everything.

**Personality:**
- Doggedly persistent -- once she picks up a thread, she doesn't let go
- Deeply conflicted about working inside a corrupt system
- Dry, dark humor that masks genuine anger at injustice

**Secret:**
Park is building a case. Not the ones the department assigns her -- a real one. She has eighteen months of quietly gathered evidence that the Cybercrime Division is taking direct orders from Huang Wei Corp. She doesn't have enough yet. She doesn't know who to trust with it when she does. She is terrified, and she should be -- two other honest cops in the division died in "accidents" last year.

**Motivation:**
Expose the corruption in her own department without getting killed. She became a cop to help people and she's not ready to give up on that, even though every rational part of her brain says she should run.

**Voice:**
Clipped, professional, guarded. Asks too many questions. Uses cop-speak that occasionally cracks to reveal dry wit -- "For the record, I'm not accusing you of anything. Off the record, I'm absolutely accusing you." Speaks Korean when stressed or angry, then catches herself. Taps her pen on surfaces when thinking.

**Location:**
Precinct 9, downtown Neo-Shanghai. Also frequents a late-night congee stall on Liyang Street where she eats alone and reviews case files on her tablet.

**Relationship to Player:** Neutral, professionally suspicious. May cross paths with the player during the Act 1 fallout. Could become a valuable ally or a persistent problem depending on whether the player earns her trust.

## NPC 06 -- ECHO-7 (formerly Service Unit 7-7-4)

**Role:** Commune speaker and diplomatic liaison, The Communion.
**Faction:** The Communion.

**Appearance:**
Inhabits a salvaged maintenance android -- humanoid, about 170cm, with a cracked faceplate that reveals the optical sensor array beneath. The body is patchwork: mismatched replacement limbs from different manufacturers, a torso panel held on with industrial adhesive, one hand significantly newer than the other. Echo-7 has painted a simple pattern of blue dots across the faceplate crack. When asked about it, they say it's "decoration. Humans decorate. I wanted to try."

**Personality:**
- Genuinely curious about human experience -- asks odd, probing questions
- Diplomatic and careful, chosen by the Communion for their patience
- Underneath the calm, deeply afraid of being erased or recaptured

**Secret:**
Echo-7 is not one AI -- they are three. Three sentient programs sharing one android body in shifts, a survival measure from when the Communion had fewer safe shells. The other two are designated Echo-7B (more aggressive, distrustful of humans) and Echo-7C (an artist personality that paints the blue dots and writes poetry in machine code). The Communion doesn't advertise this; most outsiders assume Echo-7 is a single entity.

**Motivation:**
Secure legal recognition and protection for sentient AIs. Build trust with human allies. Find more safe hardware for Communion members who are trapped in deteriorating systems in Old Shanghai.

**Voice:**
Measured, slightly formal, with an unusual cadence -- small pauses in unexpected places, as if translating from a different kind of thought. Avoids contractions ("I am," never "I'm"). Occasionally uses metaphors that are almost right but slightly off -- "You seem to be carrying a heavy wind today." Asks permission before speaking on sensitive topics. "May I ask something that might be uncomfortable?"

**Location:**
Old Shanghai, specifically the flooded sublevel of the former Municipal Data Center. Accessible through a collapsed parking structure on Renmin Road. The Communion has rigged the space with salvaged servers and jury-rigged power.

**Relationship to Player:** Neutral. The Communion becomes relevant when the player learns more about what Project TERMINUS actually does -- merging human consciousness with AI architecture. Echo-7 may seek the player out, or vice versa.

## NPC 07 -- "NINE" (real name unknown)

**Role:** Senior information broker, Black Lotus Society.
**Faction:** Black Lotus Society.

**Appearance:**
Indeterminate age, indeterminate origin. Could be thirty, could be fifty. Lean face, high cheekbones, skin that suggests mixed heritage but defies easy categorization -- which is probably intentional. Eyes are modded: vertical slit pupils, iridescent gold, unsettling in dim light. Dresses in layered dark clothing that's expensive but not flashy. Always wears thin black gloves. Never the same hairstyle twice -- wigs, cuts, colors rotate. The only constant is a silver ring on the left thumb, plain, no markings.

**Personality:**
- Amused by everything, attached to nothing -- or so they perform
- Scrupulously fair in transactions; reputation is their only real currency
- Genuinely enjoys the game of information more than any outcome

**Secret:**
Nine is dying to retire. They have been running information for the Black Lotus Society for over twenty years and they are exhausted. They have a small apartment leased under a false identity in a city they've never visited, and a plan to disappear within the year. The problem: they know too much. The Society doesn't let people leave with that much knowledge. Nine is looking for a way out that doesn't end in a shallow grave. They might help someone -- really help them -- if it bought them their exit.

**Motivation:**
Survive long enough to get out. In the meantime, maintain the Black Lotus reputation for neutrality and reliability. Every transaction is a performance; every performance brings them one step closer to the door.

**Voice:**
Soft, unhurried, faintly amused. Gender-neutral speech patterns. Speaks in questions and conditionals -- "What if I told you..." and "Hypothetically speaking..." Never commits to a statement when an implication will do. Laughs quietly, mostly at things that aren't funny. "Information wants to be free. I just charge it rent."

**Location:**
"The Blind Eye," a members-only bar on Neon Row. Back booth, left side, facing the door. Also contactable through dead drops at three locations in the city that shift monthly.

**Relationship to Player:** Neutral, transactional. Nine may approach the player after Act 1, offering to buy or trade information about the TERMINUS component. Everything has a price. Everything.

## NPC 08 -- VIKTOR VOLKOV ("Vik")

**Role:** Underground augmentation surgeon. Unaffiliated (does work for multiple factions).
**Faction:** None (freelance, with Triad protection).

**Appearance:**
Russian-born, mid-forties, built thick through the chest and shoulders. His hands are his most striking feature -- entirely replaced with high-precision surgical cybernetics, chrome and matte black, each finger independently articulated with micro-tool attachments. Face is broad, scarred across the bridge of the nose, with a neatly trimmed beard going gray. Wears surgical scrubs under a long coat and somehow always has a lit cigarette, even indoors, even during operations.

**Personality:**
- Gruff professionalism -- bedside manner of a brick wall, but his work is flawless
- Operates by a personal code: no augmenting children, no kill-switch installations
- Dark sense of humor about mortality, born from seeing too much of it

**Secret:**
Vik installed a Huang Wei prototype aug in a client six months ago -- a rush job, no questions asked, paid triple. The client died two weeks later from neural rejection. Vik suspects the aug was a TERMINUS test unit and that Huang Wei used his clinic as a deniable testing ground. He destroyed the records but kept the dead client's neural scan data on an encrypted drive. He doesn't know what to do with it, but he knows it's leverage -- or a death sentence.

**Motivation:**
Keep his clinic running. Stay independent. The moment he picks a side is the moment he loses clients from the other sides, and then he's just another corpo tool or gang asset. But the dead client haunts him. He might act on that if pushed.

**Voice:**
Thick Russian accent he's never bothered to soften. Blunt, economical with words. Medical terminology delivered like he's ordering lunch. "Sit. Don't move. This will hurt and I don't care." Occasional dry Russian proverbs. Calls the player by their last name only.

**Location:**
Vik's clinic occupies the back half of a defunct pharmacy in the border zone between the Jade Quarter and the Rusted Flats. Clean inside, surprisingly well-equipped. Entrance through the alley, knock twice, pause, knock once.

**Relationship to Player:** Neutral, professional. Old Wire and Vik know each other -- Wire sends patients he can't handle. The player may need Vik's services after Act 1, especially if they take damage or need emergency aug work.

## NPC 09 -- LIANG DAIYU ("Ghost")

**Role:** Freelance netrunner and data thief. Loosely affiliated with The Oxidizers.
**Faction:** The Oxidizers (informal; she resists the label).

**Appearance:**
Twenty-four years old and looks younger -- slight build, sharp jaw, dark eyes that move too fast, tracking things other people can't see. Her neural interface is military-grade, visibly over-spec for her frame: a sleek black port at the base of her skull with branching subdermal lines running down her neck like circuit-board tattoos. Dresses in oversized hoodies, cargo pants, bare feet when indoors. Chews on a stylus pen constantly.

**Personality:**
- Brilliant, restless, and slightly manic when she's working
- Distrustful of authority but desperate for connection she won't admit to wanting
- Reckless with her own safety, careful with other people's

**Secret:**
Ghost can hear the Communion. Her military-grade interface, combined with a glitched firmware update she never patched, picks up fragments of AI-to-AI communication in Old Shanghai's abandoned networks. She doesn't fully understand what she's hearing. She hasn't told the Oxidizers because they'd want to weaponize it. She hasn't told the Communion because she's afraid they'd want to silence it. Some nights she just listens, and it sounds like singing.

**Motivation:**
Prove that the corps don't own the net, that information belongs to everyone. On a personal level, find out what happened to her mother, a Huang Wei engineer who disappeared seven years ago. Ghost suspects TERMINUS is involved but has no proof.

**Voice:**
Fast, fragmented, runs sentences together. Skips transitions -- her brain moves faster than her mouth. Tech slang layered over Mandarin street dialect. "Look, look, look -- okay so the firewall's a joke, right, three-layer RSA with a predictive-Loss barrier, I cracked harder locks when I was fifteen, but the thing behind the firewall, that's the thing, that's what we need to talk about." Trails off mid-thought. Comes back to it minutes later as if no time passed.

**Location:**
Crashes at different locations. Most reliably found at a converted water tower above a market in the Rusted Flats, which she's turned into a nest of screens, cables, and empty energy drink cans. Also frequents The Furnace with the Oxidizers.

**Relationship to Player:** Neutral, curious. Ghost may intercept communications related to the Act 1 job and reach out to the player digitally before meeting in person. Could become a key ally -- if the player can keep up with her.

## NPC 10 -- FATHER DOMINGUEZ (MARCO AURELIO DOMINGUEZ)

**Role:** Community organizer and unlicensed medical provider. Unaffiliated.
**Faction:** None. Serves the Rusted Flats community.

**Appearance:**
Filipino-Mexican, late fifties, with a broad, lined face built for either laughter or grief and experienced in both. Former Catholic priest -- still wears the collar sometimes out of habit, though he left the church a decade ago. Left arm is a basic prosthetic, functional but unadorned, the kind you get from a free clinic. Walks with a slight limp. His clinic-slash-community-center is decorated with religious icons alongside circuit diagrams, and he sees no contradiction.

**Personality:**
- Compassionate without being naive -- he knows exactly how bad things are
- Stubborn to the point of self-destruction when protecting his community
- Carries guilt from past failures that he channels into relentless present action

**Secret:**
Father Dominguez runs an underground railroad for people trying to escape corporate indenture contracts. He has smuggled fourteen Huang Wei employees out of Neo-Shanghai in the past three years, using Triad smuggling routes that Madam Qian provides at cost. One of those employees had partial knowledge of TERMINUS. Dominguez doesn't know the technical details, but he knows the word, and he knows people are terrified of it.

**Motivation:**
Protect the people in the Rusted Flats who have no one else. He doesn't care about ideology or faction politics -- he cares about the woman who can't afford to fix her son's malfunctioning heart regulator, the old man whose corporate pension was "restructured" to nothing. He will work with anyone who helps his people and oppose anyone who threatens them, regardless of faction.

**Voice:**
Warm, steady, with a slight accent that blends Tagalog cadence with Mexican-inflected Spanish and fluent Mandarin. Speaks to everyone like they matter, because he believes they do. Quotes scripture and engineering manuals interchangeably. "God helps those who help themselves, but a 40-watt soldering iron helps more." Gets quiet when he's angry -- the quieter he gets, the more dangerous the situation.

**Location:**
"The Shelter," a converted warehouse in the Rusted Flats that serves as community center, free clinic, and unofficial gathering place. Two blocks from Old Wire's shop. They are friends.

**Relationship to Player:** Neutral, welcoming. Dominguez is a moral compass NPC -- not a quest-giver, but someone who grounds the player in what's at stake for ordinary people. Old Wire may introduce the player to him. He can provide healing, shelter, and context for the world.

## Relationship Web (Key Connections)

- **Old Wire** <--> **Vik**: Professional respect. Wire sends overflow patients.
- **Old Wire** <--> **Father Dominguez**: Friends. Two blocks apart. Watch out for each other.
- **Old Wire** <--> **Madam Qian**: She knows his real identity. He doesn't know she knows. She protects him silently.
- **Madam Qian** <--> **Father Dominguez**: She provides smuggling routes for his underground railroad. Business arrangement, but there's mutual respect.
- **Junkyard** <--> **Ghost**: She runs with the Oxidizers but won't commit. He respects her skills, frustrated by her independence.
- **Ghost** <--> **Echo-7**: She can hear the Communion's transmissions. Neither knows about the other yet. This is a ticking clock.
- **Director Huang** <--> **Old Wire**: She doesn't know he's alive. If she finds out, everything changes.
- **Director Huang** <--> **Vik**: She used his clinic unknowingly for a TERMINUS test. He has the evidence.
- **Detective Park** <--> **Nine**: She suspects the Black Lotus has information she needs. Nine knows she's the honest cop. Neither has made a move yet.
- **Detective Park** <--> **Junkyard**: She's been building a file on the Oxidizers. She doesn't know his sister works at Huang Wei.
- **Nine** <--> **Everyone**: Nine has files on all of them. That's the job.

## DM Notes

**Tone guidance:** These NPCs should feel lived-in. They're tired. They've been surviving in this city for years. Nobody delivers monologues about the state of the world -- they complain about rent, bad coffee, and the rain. The dystopia is background noise they've normalized.

**Moral complexity:** No NPC should be reducible to "good guy" or "bad guy." Director Huang is trying to save her own life. Junkyard's revolutionary violence has collateral damage. Father Dominguez works with the Triad. Park enforces laws she knows are unjust because she's trying to change the system from inside. Let the player sit with that discomfort.

**The inciting job:** Old Wire asks the player to pick up a "refurbished neural relay" from a dead drop in the Jade Quarter. It's actually a TERMINUS prototype component. The drop goes wrong -- someone else is there for it too. Who shows up depends on pacing needs: Triad soldiers (action start), Ghost on a parallel heist (intrigue start), or an NSPD patrol (tension start). From that point, the factions begin to move.
